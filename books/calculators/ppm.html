⚠️ Surgical strategies to consider for severe PPM:
  • Supra-annular prosthesis (larger effective orifice within same annulus)
  • Stentless bioprosthesis (Freestyle, Prima Plus)
  • Sutureless valve (Perceval, Intuity) — may allow larger size
  • Annular enlargement (Nicks, Manouguian, Konno procedures)
  • Transcatheter aortic valve implantation (TAVI) — generally lower PPM rates
  • Mechanical prosthesis — typically larger EOA than stented tissue at same size
Feasibility depends on patient characteristics, surgical anatomy, and individual risk. Severity should be weighed against patient age, activity level, LV function, and surgical risk.
🅿 TAVI note: Transcatheter valves generally have larger EOAs than equivalently-sized surgical prostheses and show lower predicted PPM rates, particularly in small annuli. However, post-TAVI EOA values vary substantially by valve type, size, and implant depth.

💡 Pressure recovery: In small aortic roots, pressure recovery downstream of the prosthesis may partially offset the gradient implied by a low EOA. Predicted PPM should be interpreted cautiously when the aortic root is small — net aortic pressure gradient may be clinically less severe than predicted iEOA suggests.

💡 Low-flow states: Low ejection fraction, reduced stroke volume, or atrial fibrillation may alter post-operative gradients independently of PPM. Predicted PPM does not account for haemodynamic state at the time of measurement.
📚 Reference EOA values from published hemodynamic data: Edwards INSPIRIS, Magna Ease, PERIMOUNT; Medtronic Hancock II, Mosaic, Avalus; Abbott Trifecta, Epic, St. Jude Regent/Masters; On-X mechanical; LivaNova Crown PRT, Mitroflow, Perceval (sutureless), Bicarbon and Bicarbon Slim (Sorin/LivaNova mechanical). Perceval and Bicarbon EOA values are approximate; published data varies among studies by 15–25%.
