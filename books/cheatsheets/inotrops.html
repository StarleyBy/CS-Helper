💉 Catecholamines

Click any drug to expand. First-line inotropes and vasopressors for post-cardiotomy low-output syndrome.

💉 Epinephrine (Adrenaline)

Mechanism of Action

Endogenous catecholamine acting on all adrenergic receptors (α₁, α₂, β₁, β₂) with dose-dependent potency. Increases myocardial cAMP via adenylyl cyclase → ↑Ca²⁺ influx → ↑force and rate of contraction (positive inotropy, chronotropy, lusitropy).

Receptors: β₁ ●●●● β₂ ●●● α₁ ●●● α₂ ●●

Low (<0.03 μg/kg/min) — β₁ inotropy + β₂ vasodilation → ↑CO with modest ↓SVR. Preferred post-CPB starting point. May cause lactic acidosis via β₂ glycogenolysis — not hypoperfusion.
Moderate (0.03–0.07) — Mixed β+α. ↑HR, ↑contractility, SVR rises. Significant tachycardia. ↑MVO₂. Monitor arrhythmias.
High (>0.1 μg/kg/min) — α-dominated vasoconstriction. ↑↑SVR. End-organ ischemia, hyperglycemia, hypokalemia risk. Bridge to MCS only.

Indications

  • Common first-line inotrope in many centers for borderline CO post-CPB (practice varies; dobutamine or milrinone preferred in others)
  • Hypertrophied hearts, slow sinus node — good HR response
  • Cardiac arrest (VF, PEA, asystole) — ACLS
  • Anaphylaxis (IM or IV)
  • Bridge to IABP or ECMO in refractory cardiogenic shock

Contraindications / Cautions

  • Tachycardia (>100–110 bpm) — worsens diastolic filling
  • Active myocardial ischemia — ↑MVO₂ extends infarct
  • HOCM — ↑dynamic outflow obstruction
  • Ventricular arrhythmias — lowers VT/VF threshold
⚠️ Metabolic note: Epinephrine causes dose-dependent lactic acidosis via β₂-mediated hepatic glycogenolysis and inhibition of pyruvate dehydrogenase — not an indicator of tissue hypoperfusion. Distinguish from true ischemic lactate by clinical context and SvO₂. Lactate clearance kinetics remain useful — a rising trend despite adequate haemodynamics still warrants evaluation for graft failure, mesenteric ischaemia, or other causes.
💉 Dobutamine

Mechanism of Action

Synthetic catecholamine (racemic mixture). Net: dominant β₁ inotropy with mild β₂-mediated vasodilation; negligible net α effect. ↑cAMP → PKA → ↑Ca²⁺ transient → ↑contractility. Does NOT stimulate dopamine receptors.

  • Uncorrected hypovolemia — vasodilator drops MAP
  • AF with rapid ventricular response — can ↑rate

💉 Dopamine

Mechanism of Action

Immediate precursor of norepinephrine. Acts on DA₁, DA₂, β₁, β₂, α₁ receptors with markedly dose-dependent affinity. Also releases NE from sympathetic terminals (indirect effect).

Receptors: DA₁ ●●● (low) β₁ ●●● (mid) α₁ ●●●● (high)

Rapid onset and offset. Does not cross BBB at standard doses.

The "Three Zones" — Dose-Dependent

1–3 μg/kg/min3–10 μg/kg/min>10 μg/kg/min
DA₁: renal vasodilation
β₁: ↑CO, ↑HR
α₁: ↑↑SVR, ↑MVO₂
“Renal dose” 1–3 μg/kg/min — DA₁ renal vasodilation → ↑urine output. Does NOT confer renal protection (ANZICS 2000, multiple meta-analyses). Concept discredited for AKI prevention.
“Cardiac dose” 3–10 μg/kg/min — β₁ inotropy + chronotropy → ↑CO. Significant tachycardia and arrhythmia (PVCs, AF) more common than with epinephrine.
“Vasopressor dose” >10 μg/kg/min — Predominantly α₁ → ↑↑SVR. Visceral ischemia. NE is preferred at this range — more predictable, less arrhythmogenic.

Limited Indications (post-CS)

  • Low CO + low SVR when epi/NE unavailable
  • Oliguria — not recommended solely to improve urine output; use only if bradycardia with low CO co-exists
  • Bradycardia with hemodynamic compromise (bridge to pacing)

Relative Contraindications

  • Highest arrhythmia risk among catecholamines — avoid in AF, VT
  • Pheochromocytoma — paradoxical hypertension
  • MAO inhibitor therapy — markedly potentiated
Clinical position 2025: Dopamine largely replaced by epi + NE in cardiac surgery. SOAP-II trial: higher mortality and arrhythmia vs NE in shock. ESC/ACC guidelines prefer NE as first-line vasopressor.
💉 Isoproterenol (Isoprenaline)

Mechanism of Action

Synthetic catecholamine with pure β₁ and β₂ agonism — zero α activity. β₁: marked ↑HR and ↑contractility. β₂: ↓SVR, ↓↓PVR, bronchodilation. Dominant effect is profound tachycardia. Also ↑AV node conduction velocity (useful in complete heart block).

Receptors: β₁ ●●●●● β₂ ●●●● α₁ — (zero)

Pharmacokinetics

<1 minOnset
~2 mint½
Hepatic/lung COMTMetabolism
IV centralRoute

Extremely short t½ — ideal for precise HR titration. Not taken up by sympathetic terminals unlike other catecholamines.

Low 0.01–0.02 μg/kg/min — Modest ↑HR (+10–20 bpm), mild ↓PVR. Maintain HR 90–100 bpm post-transplant or in heart block awaiting pacemaker.
Mid 0.02–0.05 μg/kg/min — Pronounced tachycardia, ↑CO via HR. ↓↓SVR, ↓↓PVR. May need α-agonist for MAP. Major ↑MVO₂.
High >0.05 μg/kg/min — Extreme tachycardia, high VT/VF risk. Hypotension from vasodilation. Absolute bridge use only.

Indications

  • Orthotopic heart transplant — denervated heart; target HR 90–110 bpm. CO relies more heavily on heart rate than in the innervated heart — chronotropy is the primary CO determinant.
  • Post-mitral valve RV failure + ↑PVR — ↓↓PVR via β₂
  • Bradycardia (sinus, AV block) as bridge to pacing
  • Torsades de Pointes (↑HR shortens QT)

Contraindications

  • Tachycardia or tachyarrhythmias — absolute CI
  • Active myocardial ischemia — extreme ↑MVO₂
  • Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy
  • Digitalis toxicity
💉 Norepinephrine — First-line vasopressor

Mechanism of Action

Primary endogenous vasopressor. Potent α₁ and moderate β₁; minimal β₂. Dominant effect: vasoconstriction (↑SVR) via α₁ → ↑MAP. β₁ provides mild inotropy, usually offset by baroreflex bradycardia (↑MAP → ↑vagal tone). Reflex bradycardia improves diastolic filling.

Receptors: α₁ ●●●●● β₁ ●●● β₂ ●

Pharmacokinetics

1–3 minOnset
~2 mint½
Neuronal uptakeClearance
IV central onlyRoute

Extravasation causes tissue necrosis — dedicated central line mandatory.

Low 0.01–0.05 μg/kg/min — ↑SVR, ↑MAP. Mild ↑CI via improved coronary perfusion + baroreflex inotropy. First-line range for post-CPB vasoplegia with adequate CO.
Moderate 0.05–0.15 μg/kg/min — Significant vasoconstriction. If CI <2.0, must add inotrope — NE alone insufficient for CO. Mesenteric/renal vasoconstriction risk.
High >0.2 μg/kg/min — Severe vasoconstriction. Visceral ischemia, digital necrosis, metabolic acidosis. Add vasopressin, consider methylene blue, evaluate underlying cause.

Indications

  • Vasoplegic syndrome post-CPB — low SVR + adequate CO
  • Low CO + low SVR combined with inotrope
  • Septic shock (SOAP-II: ↓arrhythmia, ↓mortality vs dopamine)
  • Rebound hypotension after milrinone loading

Contraindications / Cautions

  • Uncorrected hypovolemia — vasoconstriction without volume = ↓CO
  • Arterial graft spasm risk (ITA, radial) — maintain diltiazem/NTG
  • Elevated PVR: excessive NE doses may ↑PVR. However, NE is not contraindicated in RV failure — improved MAP benefits RV coronary perfusion. Combine with pulmonary vasodilator if RV afterload elevated.
Arterial graft consideration: NE causes α₁-mediated spasm of ITA and radial conduits. When NE required, maintain diltiazem 0.1 mg/kg/h IV or NTG 0.5–1 μg/kg/min to protect graft patency.
💉 Phenylephrine — Pure α₁ agonist

Mechanism of Action

Pure selective α₁ agonist — no β activity. Dose-dependent peripheral vasoconstriction → ↑SVR → ↑MAP → reflex bradycardia (baroreceptor). No direct inotropy, chronotropy, or lusitropy. CO may fall if reflex bradycardia is significant (CO = HR × SV).

Receptors: α₁ ●●●●● β₁ — (zero) β₂ — (zero)

Pharmacokinetics

1–3 minOnset
~5 mint½

IV/IM bolusRoutes

Peripheral IV acceptable for bolus; continuous infusion needs central line. Slightly longer offset than NE.

Indications

  • Hypotension with adequate CI >2.2 — isolated vasodilation
  • HOCM / SAM after mitral repair — ↑SVR prevents outflow obstruction
  • Spinal/epidural hypotension
  • SVT termination via reflex bradycardia

Contraindications

  • Low CO state — pure vasoconstriction ↓CO further
  • Arterial graft conduits — highest spasm risk
  • Severe bradycardia — reflex ↓HR dangerous
  • Aortic insufficiency — ↑SVR worsens regurgitation
Central line mandatory for all vasoactive infusions. Dedicated lumen — never mix incompatible drugs in the same line. Always calculate weight-based rates.

💊 Inodilators & Special Agents

PDE-III inhibitors, non-catecholamine vasopressors, calcium sensitizers, and salvage inotropes.

💊 Milrinone (Primacor) — PDE-III Inhibitor

Mechanism of Action

Selectively inhibits phosphodiesterase type III in myocardium and vascular smooth muscle. Prevents cAMP degradation → amplifies cAMP effects: in heart → ↑Ca²⁺ cycling → positive inotropy + lusitropy; in vasculature → smooth muscle relaxation → ↓SVR, ↓↓PVR. Post-receptor mechanism — bypasses downregulated β-receptors; additive with catecholamines.

Mechanism: PDE-III inhibitor cAMP ↑↑ β-receptor independent

Pharmacokinetics

5–15 minOnset
1.5–2.5 ht½ (normal)
3–5 ht½ (renal fail)
80–85%Renal excretion

⚠️ Renal dose adjustment required: 85% renally excreted. Reduce/avoid bolus in CrCl <30 mL/min. t½ doubles in renal failure — prolonged hypotension risk after weaning.

Dosing Protocol

Loading bolus: 25–50 μg/kg over 10 min — Immediate ↓SVR, modest ↑CO. Often causes transient hypotension requiring α-agonist co-administration. Many contemporary cardiac surgery programs omit the loading dose entirely to avoid hypotension — begin maintenance infusion directly (STS / Bojar 6th ed.).
Maintenance: 0.25–0.5 μg/kg/min — ↓PCWP 15–30%, ↓PVR 20–40%, ↑CI 20–40%. Preferred range for post-CPB low-output + elevated PVR. Usually requires NE to maintain MAP.
High: 0.5–0.75 μg/kg/min — ↑Vasodilation. Arrhythmia risk (PVCs, VT) increases. Not superior to mid-range + NE combination. Accumulates in renal failure.

Indications

  • Persistent low CO despite catecholamine support
  • RV failure with elevated PVR — post-mitral, transplant, PH
  • Diastolic dysfunction — improves LV compliance (lusitropy)
  • Coronary/conduit spasm (ITA, radial) — smooth muscle relaxation
  • Inhaled milrinone: selective pulmonary vasodilation without systemic effects

Contraindications / Cautions

  • Severe hypotension without vasopressor support
  • Significant renal failure — halve dose, avoid loading bolus
  • HOCM — outflow tract obstruction
  • AF with rapid ventricular response — ↑AV conduction
  • Pre-existing ventricular arrhythmias
  • Chronic oral PDE inhibitors (sildenafil, tadalafil) — attenuated response
⚠️ OPTIME-CHF trial: Milrinone ↑arrhythmias and hypotension without mortality benefit in decompensated HF. In post-cardiotomy low-output with high PVR, milrinone remains the preferred inodilator. Inhaled milrinone (5 mg nebulized) provides selective pulmonary vasodilation with minimal systemic effects.
💉 Vasopressin (ADH / AVP) — Non-catecholamine vasopressor

Mechanism of Action

Acts on V₁ receptors in vascular smooth muscle → Gq → IP₃ → ↑intracellular Ca²⁺ → vasoconstriction. Entirely independent of adrenergic receptors — effective in catecholamine-refractory shock. At therapeutic doses, does NOT increase PVR (selective systemic vasculature) — advantage in RV failure.

Receptors: V₁ ●●●●● (vascular) V₂ ●● (renal) α/β — (none)

Pharmacokinetics

2–5 minOnset
10–35 mint½
VasopressinaseEnzymes
IV centralRoute

Longer t½ than catecholamines — slower offset when weaning. Endogenous depletion occurs in prolonged vasoplegic shock (rationale for exogenous replacement).

Physiologic: 0.01–0.04 U/min — Restores endogenous AVP. ↑MAP with ↓NE requirements. No significant mesenteric ischemia risk. VASST trial dose range.
Moderate: 0.04–0.1 U/min — Significant vasoconstriction. Mesenteric, renal, and cutaneous flow ↓. CO may ↓ if LV afterload increases significantly.
High: >0.1 U/min — Severe splanchnic ischemia. Myocardial ischemia (coronary V₁). Hepatic necrosis reported. Rarely indicated.

Indications

  • Vasoplegic syndrome refractory to catecholamines — post-CPB, protamine reaction
  • NE dose reduction (catecholamine-sparing)
  • RV failure + high PVR — preferred (doesn't ↑PVR)
  • Diabetes insipidus post-operative (V₂-ADH replacement)

Contraindications

  • Low CO without inotrope — pure vasoconstriction ↓CO
  • Mesenteric ischemia risk — monitor bowel, lactate
  • CAD — coronary vasoconstriction at high doses
  • Hyponatraemia — V₂ water retention
Pharmacological rationale: In prolonged vasodilatory shock, endogenous AVP stores deplete (t½ of depletion ~1–2h). Exogenous vasopressin at 0.03 U/min restores plasma levels to normal — replacement therapy, not pharmacologic vasoconstriction. VASST trial: non-inferior to NE with lower NE requirements.
⚡ Levosimendan (Simdax) — Calcium Sensitizer + Inodilator

Dual Mechanism of Action

(1) Calcium sensitization: binds troponin C in a Ca²⁺-dependent manner → stabilizes TnC-Ca²⁺ complex during systole → ↑contractile force without ↑intracellular Ca²⁺ → no ↑MVO₂. (2) KATP channel opening: vasodilation (↓SVR, ↓↓PVR) + cardioprotection. Active metabolite OR-1896 (t½ 75–80h) perpetuates effects for 7–9 days after 24h infusion.

Mechanism: TnC sensitizer KATP opener PDE-III (mild)

Pharmacokinetics

5–10 minOnset
~1 ht½ parent
75–80 ht½ OR-1896
7–9 daysDuration effect

Prolonged effect of OR-1896 means effects last up to a week. Cannot be rapidly reversed if hypotension develops.

Loading: 12–24 μg/kg over 10 min — Rapid ↑CO, ↓PCWP. Often causes transient hypotension. Consider omitting if SBP <90 or STEMI context.
Maintenance: 0.05–0.2 μg/kg/min × 24h — ↑CI 25–40%, ↓SVR 20–30%, ↓PVR 30–40%. No ↑MVO₂. Most patients need NE throughout.
Post-infusion (OR-1896): 24h–9 days — Benefit persists. Plasma NE falls (↓catecholamine dependence). Monitor for delayed hypotension if vasopressors weaned too quickly.

Indications

  • Pre-operative optimization of high-risk cardiac surgery
  • Post-cardiotomy RV or LV failure — ↓PVR + ↑RV contractility
  • Weaning from CPB in severely impaired LV (EF <30%)
  • β-receptor downregulated HF patients
  • Heart transplant — post-reperfusion primary graft dysfunction

Contraindications

  • Severe hypotension (SBP <85) without vasopressor support
  • Significant hypovolemia — vasodilation ↓↓MAP
  • Severe renal failure (CrCl <15) — OR-1896 accumulation
  • Hypokalemia — KATP opening ↑arrhythmia risk; correct K⁺ first
Key advantage: Only inotrope that improves contractility without increasing MVO₂ (Ca²⁺ sensitization ≠ ↑Ca²⁺ cycling). Uniquely suitable for ischemic hearts where catecholamine-induced Ca²⁺ overload could extend injury.
⚡ Calcium Chloride (CaCl₂)

Mechanism

IV calcium ↑ plasma ionized Ca²⁺ → ↑Ca²⁺ influx via L-type channels → ↑SR release → transient ↑contractility. Also ↑SVR via vascular smooth muscle. In post-ischemic/reperfused heart, intracellular Ca²⁺ already elevated — additional Ca²⁺ may worsen reperfusion injury.

1–2 minOnset
15–30 minDuration
272 mg/gElemental Ca (CaCl₂)
90 mg/gElemental Ca (gluconate)

Evidence — Controversial

Routine post-CPB use NOT recommended. Multiple studies show: (1) Transient ↑contractility equivalent to catecholamines; (2) May worsen reperfusion injury in stunned myocardium; (3) No mortality benefit. Many centers abandoned routine CaCl₂ at CPB separation.
CaCl₂ vs Gluconate: CaCl₂ preferred for immediate effect — no hepatic metabolism needed for ionized Ca²⁺ release. Use CaCl₂ in emergencies; gluconate for prophylaxis/maintenance.
1. Hypocalcemia — Ionized Ca²⁺ <1.1 mmol/L (common post-CPB: citrate, hemodilution, hypothermia). True indication: replace to normal before calling for inotropes.
2. Hyperkalemia — K⁺ >6.0–6.5 mEq/L: calcium stabilizes myocardial membrane. Acts 1–2 min, duration 30 min. Does not treat underlying hyperkalemia.
3. CCB toxicity — IV CaCl₂ reverses negative inotropy/chronotropy from CCB overdose. High repeated doses may be needed.
ACLS 2025: Routine calcium in cardiac arrest NOT recommended (Class III: harm) unless hyperkalemia, hypocalcemia, or CCB toxicity. Measure ionized Ca²⁺ first — replace only if <1.1 mmol/L.
⚡ Triiodothyronine (T₃) — Salvage Inotrope

Mechanism of Action

T₃ (liothyronine) acts on nuclear thyroid hormone receptors → ↑transcription of: α-myosin heavy chain, SERCA2a (↑Ca²⁺ reuptake), β₁-adrenergic receptors. Mechanism is entirely β-receptor independent — works even in fully downregulated states. Cardiac surgery causes T₃ to fall 50–70% within hours (“euthyroid sick syndrome”).

Mechanism: Nuclear TH receptor SERCA2a ↑ β₁ upregulation

Indications

  • Salvage: IABP + maximal catecholamines + milrinone insufficient
  • Refractory low CO with suspected T₃ deficiency post-CPB
  • Brain-dead donor heart preparation
  • Neonatal/pediatric cardiac surgery

Limitations

  • Randomized data limited; modest benefit in trials
  • Tachycardia and arrhythmia risk at higher doses
  • Drug availability limited in many countries
  • Not a substitute for MCS when truly indicated

🎯 Phenotype-Guided Drug Selection

Choose by hemodynamic phenotype (CI, SVR, MAP) — not by drug name. Volume-optimize first.

📉 Low CI + High SVR
Cold, clamped — adequate or high BP
  • 1st: Dobutamine 5–10 μg/kg/min
  • Alt: Milrinone 0.25–0.5 μg/kg/min
  • If MAP ↓: Add NE 0.03–0.05 μg/kg/min
  • Refractory: Levosimendan + NE
📉 Low CI + Low SVR
Cold, vasodilated — low BP
  • 1st: Epinephrine 1–4 μg/min
  • Combination: Dobutamine + NE
  • Severe: Epi + NE + Vasopressin
  • MCS: IABP / ECMO if refractory
↔ Normal CI + Low SVR
Warm, vasodilated — vasoplegia
  • 1st: Norepinephrine 0.01–0.1 μg/kg/min
  • 2nd: Add Vasopressin 0.03 U/min
  • 3rd: Methylene blue 1–2 mg/kg IV
  • Exclude: Anaphylaxis, adrenal crisis
🫀 RV Failure + ↑PVR
RV dilation — ↑CVP, low CO, ↑PVR
  • 1st: Milrinone ± inhaled milrinone
  • Add: Isoproterenol (↓PVR, ↑HR)
  • Pulmonary: iNO / inhaled prostacyclin
  • Systemic: Low-dose NE if MAP low

📐 Stepwise Vasoactive Drug Selection — Post-Cardiotomy
0

Optimize before pharmacology

Volume: CVP 8–12, PCWP 12–16 mmHg. Correct rhythm (brady/tachy). Pacing 80–90 bpm if slow. Temperature ≥36.5°C. Ionized Ca²⁺ ≥1.1 mmol/L. Hb ≥7–8 g/dL. Rule out tamponade (echo).

1

CI ≥2.2, MAP <70 — Vasodilatory (vasoplegia)

First: Norepinephrine 0.01–0.1 μg/kg/min
Refractory: Add Vasopressin 0.01–0.04 U/min
Graft concern: Maintain diltiazem/NTG
Refractory: Methylene blue 1–2 mg/kg IV

2

CI <2.2 — Low output state

First-line: Epinephrine 1–4 μg/min — best balance of inotropy vs tachycardia
Alternative: Dobutamine 5–10 μg/kg/min if SVR elevated
Tachycardia limiting: Prefer milrinone over dobutamine

3

CI <2.2 despite catecholamines

Add Milrinone: 25–50 μg/kg bolus → 0.25–0.5 μg/kg/min (synergistic; add NE to prevent hypotension)
Consider Levosimendan if ischemic etiology or β-receptor downregulation: 12 μg/kg load → 0.1 μg/kg/min × 24h

4

Refractory low output (CI <1.8 despite above)

IABP — ↑diastolic perfusion, ↓afterload; most immediate MCS option
T₃ salvage: 0.8 μg/kg IV if euthyroid sick syndrome suspected
Impella CP / VA-ECMO — if no improvement with IABP in 1–2h
Re-exploration to exclude surgical cause, tamponade, tension PTX

Special Situations

RV failure + ↑PVR
Milrinone (± inhaled) + Isoproterenol or Levosimendan + Inhaled NO/prostacyclin. Phenylephrine: avoid in isolated RV failure (no inotropy, ↑RV afterload). NE: use cautiously — improved MAP often benefits RV coronary perfusion; combine with pulmonary vasodilator. Target: PVR <240 dyn·s/cm⁵, RV-PA coupling ratio >0.7.
Vasoplegic syndrome (warm, high CO, low BP)
NE first-line + Vasopressin 0.03 U/min. Refractory: Methylene blue 1.5 mg/kg IV over 1h. Exclude protamine reaction, anaphylaxis, adrenal insufficiency.
ITA / radial artery graft spasm risk
Minimise α-agonists. Maintain diltiazem 0.1 mg/kg/h IV. NTG 0.5–1 μg/kg/min. If vasopressor needed: NE < phenylephrine for spasm risk (NE has β₁ co-activation that ↑graft flow).
Tachycardia + low output
Prefer milrinone ± low-dose epinephrine (<2 μg/min). Rate control if tolerated. If AF: amiodarone or cardioversion before increasing inotropes.
Post-transplant denervated heart
Isoproterenol first-line (target HR 90–110 bpm — CO is HR-dependent). Milrinone for PVR. Atropine and digoxin ineffective on rate (denervated). Early CRT if bradycardia persists.
Weaning order
① Wean α-agonists first (as patient warms, SVR normalises) → ② ↓catecholamines → ③ Wean milrinone last (t½ 2h — halve every 4h). IABP removal: CI ≥2.2 + single low-dose inotrope.

Daily SICU Monitoring Checklist

  • CI / SvO₂ / ScvO₂ — trend every 4h or continuous PA catheter
  • MAP — target 65–85 mmHg (CAD: ≥70)
  • HR — target 70–90 bpm; >100 = reassess drug choice
  • Urine output — ≥0.5 mL/kg/h
  • Lactate — normal <2 mmol/L; rising = ↓CO or ↑vasoconstriction
  • Ionized Ca²⁺ — replace if <1.1 mmol/L
  • K⁺ — replace to ≥3.5 mEq/L
  • Temperature — cool patients have ↑SVR; warm to normothermia
  • Hemoglobin — maintain ≥7 g/dL (higher in ischemic HD)
  • Assess volume status before increasing any inotrope

Weaning Strategy (Bojar 6th ed.)

1. ↓ phenylephrine / vasopressin first (as patient warms, SVR normalises)
2. Reduce NE when MAP stable >70
3. Taper epi to ≤1–2 μg/min; dobu to ≤5 μg/kg/min
4. Wean milrinone last (t½ 2h) — halve every 4–6h; watch for rebound ↑SVR
5. IABP removal: CI ≥2.2, single low-dose inotrope, MAP stable ≥70
6. Levosimendan: no active weaning (OR-1896 self-resolves 7–9 days)
