# Cirrhosis — Quick Reference

## Child-Pugh Score

| Parameter | 1 point | 2 points | 3 points |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bilirubin | < 34 µmol/L | 34–51 | > 51 |
| Albumin | > 35 g/L | 28–35 | < 28 |
| PT prolongation | < 4 sec | 4–6 sec | > 6 sec |
| Ascites | None | Mild/moderate | Severe |
| Encephalopathy | None | Grade I–II | Grade III–IV |

| Class | Score | 1-yr survival | 2-yr survival |
|:---:|:---:|---|---|
| A | 5–6 | 100% | 85% |
| B | 7–9 | 80% | 60% |
| C | 10–15 | 45% | 35% |

## MELD Score

**MELD = 3.78 × ln[Bilirubin mg/dL] + 11.2 × ln[INR] + 9.57 × ln[Creatinine mg/dL] + 6.43**

| MELD | 90-day mortality |
|:---:|---|
| < 9 | ~2% |
| 10–19 | ~6% |
| 20–29 | ~20% |
| 30–39 | ~53% |
| ≥ 40 | ~71% |

## Complications & Management

### Ascites
- **Grade 1:** Dietary Na restriction (< 2 g/day)
- **Grade 2:** Spironolactone 100 mg/day + Furosemide 40 mg/day (ratio 5:2); titrate
- **Grade 3 (tense):** Large volume paracentesis + albumin 8 g per liter removed; continue diuretics
- **Refractory:** TIPS; consider transplant

### Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis (SBP)
- Diagnosis: PMN > 250/mm³ in ascitic fluid
- Treatment: Cefotaxime 2 g IV q8h × 5 days (or Amoxicillin-Clavulanate PO)
- Albumin: 1.5 g/kg on Day 1 + 1 g/kg on Day 3 → prevents hepatorenal syndrome
- **Primary prophylaxis:** Norfloxacin if protein < 15 g/L or Child-Pugh ≥ 9

### Hepatic Encephalopathy
- **Grade I:** Mild confusion, reversed sleep-wake cycle
- **Grade II:** Lethargy, asterixis
- **Grade III:** Somnolence, marked confusion
- **Grade IV:** Coma
- **Treatment:** Lactulose (titrate to 2–3 soft stools/day) + Rifaximin 550 mg BID
- Identify and treat precipitants (infection, GI bleed, constipation, sedatives, electrolyte imbalance)

### Variceal Bleeding
- **Acute:** Octreotide 50 mcg bolus → 50 mcg/h infusion × 72h; Ceftriaxone 1 g/day
- **Endoscopy:** Band ligation within 12h
- **Rescue:** TIPS for failure to control or early rebleed
- **Secondary prevention:** Non-selective beta-blocker (Propranolol / Carvedilol) + band ligation

### Hepatorenal Syndrome (HRS)
- **Type 1 (AKI):** Creatinine doubles to > 226 µmol/L in < 2 weeks
- **Type 2 (CKD pattern):** Slower, associated with refractory ascites
- **Treatment:** Terlipressin 1 mg q4–6h + Albumin 1 g/kg Day 1 then 20–40 g/day; target MAP ↑ ≥ 15 mmHg

## Drug Cautions in Cirrhosis

| Drug | Issue |
|---|---|
| NSAIDs | Risk of AKI, GI bleed — **avoid** |
| Aminoglycosides | Nephrotoxic — avoid |
| Benzodiazepines | Precipitate encephalopathy — minimize |
| Metformin | Risk of lactic acidosis if Child B/C |
| Statins | Use cautiously; myopathy risk |
| Beta-blockers | Use non-selective for portal HTN; avoid if SBP < 90 or AKI |
| Warfarin | Unpredictable response; INR unreliable for dosing |
