# Acute Kidney Injury — Quick Reference

## KDIGO Staging

| Stage | Serum Creatinine | Urine Output |
|:---:|---|---|
| 1 | ×1.5–1.9 baseline OR ↑ ≥ 26.5 µmol/L in 48h | < 0.5 mL/kg/h for 6–12h |
| 2 | ×2.0–2.9 baseline | < 0.5 mL/kg/h for ≥ 12h |
| 3 | ×3.0 baseline OR Cr ≥ 354 µmol/L OR RRT | < 0.3 mL/kg/h for ≥ 24h OR anuria ≥ 12h |

## Etiology Framework

### Pre-renal
- Hypovolemia: bleeding, dehydration, burns, third-spacing
- Low effective arterial volume: HF, cirrhosis, sepsis
- Renovascular: RAS, thrombosis

### Intrinsic Renal
| Compartment | Cause |
|---|---|
| Tubular (ATN) | Ischemia, contrast, aminoglycosides, myoglobin, hemolysis |
| Glomerular | GN, vasculitis, TTP/HUS |
| Interstitial | AIN (NSAIDs, PPIs, antibiotics) |
| Vascular | Atheroemboli, renal vein thrombosis |

### Post-renal
- Ureteric obstruction (stones, clot, tumor)
- Bladder outlet: BPH, stricture, neurogenic

## Workup

| Test | Pre-renal | ATN |
|---|---|---|
| FeNa | < 1% | > 2% |
| FeUrea | < 35% | > 50% |
| Urine Na | < 20 mEq/L | > 40 mEq/L |
| Urine Osm | > 500 | < 350 |
| Sediment | Normal/hyaline casts | Muddy brown granular casts |

## Indications for Urgent RRT (AEIOU)

- **A** — Acidosis: pH < 7.1 refractory to treatment
- **E** — Electrolytes: K⁺ > 6.5 or refractory hyperkalemia
- **I** — Intoxication: dialyzable toxin (methanol, ethylene glycol, lithium, salicylates)
- **O** — Overload: pulmonary edema refractory to diuretics
- **U** — Uremia: encephalopathy, pericarditis, bleeding

## Drug Dose Adjustments in AKI

| Drug | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Metformin | Hold if eGFR < 30 |
| LMWH | Use UFH if eGFR < 30 |
| Digoxin | Reduce dose; monitor levels |
| Gabapentin | Significant reduction; avoid in severe AKI |
| Acyclovir | Reduce 50–75% based on GFR |
| Methotrexate | Contraindicated in severe AKI |
| NSAIDs | **Avoid** — worsen AKI |
| Contrast | Minimize; pre-hydrate; consider iso-osmolar |

## Hyperkalemia Management

| K⁺ Level | Action |
|---|---|
| 5.5–6.0 | Low K diet, kayexalate/patiromer, treat cause |
| 6.0–6.5 + ECG changes | Calcium gluconate 10 mL 10% IV (stabilize membrane) |
| > 6.5 / severe ECG changes | Ca gluconate + Insulin 10U + Glucose 50g + Salbutamol 10–20 mg nebulized; prepare for RRT |

## Contrast Nephropathy Prevention

- IV NS 0.9% 1 mL/kg/h for 6–12h before and after
- Use minimum contrast volume; iso-osmolar preferred
- Hold NSAIDs, metformin 24–48h peri-procedure
- NAC evidence weak — not routinely recommended
