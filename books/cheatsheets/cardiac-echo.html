🫀 Right heart — 2025 ASE grading & hemodynamics

RV size (basal diameter)

  • Normal: < 4.1 cm
  • Mild: 4.1–4.4 cm
  • Moderate: >4.4–4.9 cm
  • Severe: >4.9 cm

RV wall thickness

  • Normal: < 0.5 cm
  • Mild: 0.5–0.7 cm
  • Moderate: >0.7–0.9 cm
  • Severe: >0.9 cm

RA area

  • Normal: < 19 cm²
  • Mild: 19–22 cm²
  • Moderate: >22–24 cm²
  • Severe: >24 cm²

TAPSE (RV systolic function)

  • Normal: ≥ 17 mm
  • S' wave (TDI): ≥ 9.5 cm/s (2025 ASE: mild 9.5–7.2, moderate 7.2–5.0, severe <5.0 cm/s)
📊 Pulmonary hypertension probability: TRV ≤ 2.8 m/s + no other signs → low; TRV 2.9–3.4 m/s → intermediate; TRV >3.4 m/s → high probability. mPAP threshold now >20 mmHg (2025 ASE/ERS).

Mitral regurgitation (MR) — Secondary (functional) — lower thresholds

Secondary MR (Carpentier IIIb) has adverse outcomes at lower regurgitant volumes. EROA ≥0.30 cm² or RVol ≥45 mL is considered severe (COAPT criteria). Always classify mechanism (I: normal motion, II: prolapse, IIIa: restricted diastole+sys, IIIb: restricted systole only).
📌 Wall-hugging eccentric jets (Coanda effect) systematically underestimate color jet area — use vena contracta and PISA for quantification.

📈 Diastolic function — 2025 ASE update, filling pressures & HFA-PEFF

Normal values / cutoffs (sinus rhythm, EF ≥50%)

  • E/A ratio: 0.8–2.0
  • E/e' average (septal + lateral): < 14
  • LA volume index: < 34 mL/m²
  • TR velocity: < 2.8 m/s

2025 ASE grading (4‑step algorithm)

  • Grade I (impaired relaxation): E/A <0.8, e' ↓, deceleration time prolonged
  • Grade II (pseudonormal): E/A 0.8–2.0 + elevated filling pressure (E/e' ≥14 or LAVI ≥34)
  • Grade III (restrictive): E/A >2.0, DT <150 ms, E/e' ≥14, TR velocity >2.8
📊 Elevated LV filling pressure (LVFP) is likely if:
• Average E/e' >14
• Septal e' <7 cm/s, lateral e' <10 cm/s
• TR velocity >2.8 m/s
• LAVI >34 mL/m²
For EF <50% algorithm differs (pulmonary vein S/D ratio etc.).
🚩 Special populations: atrial fibrillation (multiple cycles), severe MR, post‑cardiac surgery (avoid strict cutoffs, use integrative approach).
🌀 Phenomena, artifacts & Doppler signatures

Common artifacts & pitfalls

  • Reverberation / comet-tail: mimics foreign bodies (e.g. pacemaker lead “ring-down”)
  • Mirror image: duplicate structures across strong reflectors (diaphragm, aortic wall)
  • Side‑lobe artifact: Doppler velocities or structures appearing out of true beam axis
  • Aliasing: CW Doppler velocities wrap around — increase scale or baseline shift
  • Color Doppler “stripes”: can mimic severe regurgitation — always align with jet direction
The first ASE guideline on cardiac ultrasound artifacts (2026) details mitigation strategies for reverberation, acoustic shadowing, and external interference (e.g. from VAD, TEE probe).

Pathognomonic Doppler/color signs

  • Coanda effect (wall‑hugging MR jet): systematically underestimates severity → use vena contracta and PISA
  • “Sandwich sign” in constrictive pericarditis: septal bounce, preserved e', annulus reversus
  • Smoke (spontaneous echo contrast): dense swirling in LA/LAA → high thrombus risk
  • Dagger‑shaped CW envelope in hypertrophic cardiomyopathy LVOT obstruction
  • Laminar vs mosaic flow: high-velocity turbulence indicates stenosis/regurgitation
🫀 Disease phenotypes — HCM, amyloid, PH, prosthetic valve

Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM)

  • Asymmetric LVH (septal >1.3× posterior wall), LVOT gradient (>30 mmHg resting/provocable)
  • “Dagger‑shaped” CW Doppler, systolic anterior motion (SAM) of mitral valve, often MR
  • Strain: reduced GLS with apical sparing pattern; LVOT obstruction assessment key for septal reduction

Cardiac amyloidosis (restrictive phenotype)

  • Increased wall thickness with low voltage ECG, “granular sparkling” myocardium
  • Reduced GLS with apical sparing (bull’s‑eye), biatrial enlargement, restrictive filling (E/A >2, DT short)
  • Associated pericardial effusion and thickened valves

Pulmonary hypertension (2025 ASE guidelines)

  • TRV probability: >3.4 m/s → high probability of PH (mPAP >20 mmHg).
  • RV remodelling (basal diameter >4.1 cm), RA area increase, TAPSE <17 mm, systolic flattening of the interventricular septum (D‑sign).
  • PA diameter >2.5 cm, RVOT acceleration time <100 ms (mid‑systolic notching).

Prosthetic valve dysfunction (PVT / pannus)

  • Increased Doppler gradient + reduced valve mobility (TEE 3D gold standard).
  • Thrombus: soft mass, reduced opening angle, often in the hinge mechanism (bileaflet).
  • Pannus: hyperechoic, immobile, usually chronic.
  • Fluoroscopy: limited leaflet motion; echo shows increased velocities above expected for prosthesis type/size.
🚨 Any sudden gradient increase in a mechanical valve warrants exclusion of obstructive thrombosis with TEE.
