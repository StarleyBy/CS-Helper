🫀 CALS – CARDIAC ADVANCED LIFE SUPPORT (2025) POST‑STERNOTOMY ARREST PROTOCOL

Specialized protocol for cardiac arrest after median sternotomy. Adheres to STS, EACTS, ERC guidelines.
Key differences from ACLS: prioritise defibrillation/epicardial pacing, limited external chest compressions, emergency resternotomy within <5 min.
🆘 CALS ALGORITHM — CARDIAC ARREST AFTER CARDIAC SURGERY
1️⃣ RECOGNITION (0–10 sec)
  • Loss of pulse on arterial line + loss of SpO₂ / invasive pressure waveform.
  • Simultaneously shout “Cardiac arrest!” → activate cardiac surgery resuscitation team (surgeon, perfusionist).
2️⃣ IMMEDIATE THERAPY (first 60 sec, CHEST COMPRESSIONS MAY BE DEFERRED)
VF / PULSELESS VT → 3 stacked shocks (if immediate readiness) 150–200 J biphasic
BRADYARRHYTHMIA / ASYSTOLE → Activate epicardial pacing: DDD / asynchronous, rate 100 bpm, max mA/V output.
PEA → Immediately rule out tamponade / hypovolemia / pneumothorax (consider POCUS).
📌 ACLS deviation: External chest compressions may be deferred for up to 60 sec if defibrillation or pacing can be delivered rapidly. External compressions are dangerous in tamponade and fresh sternotomy (risk of RV rupture).
3️⃣ EXTERNAL CHEST COMPRESSIONS (if no response or cause not reversible)
  • 100–120 /min, depth 5–6 cm, target invasive SBP ≥60 mmHg.
  • Use backboard, minimise interruptions.
  • Simultaneously prepare for EMERGENCY RESTERNOTOMY (must be done within 5 min of arrest).
4️⃣ EMERGENCY RESTERNOTOMY (time is survival)

Indications: no ROSC after defibrillation/pacing, suspected tamponade, massive bleeding, ineffective external compressions.
Timing: resternotomy within 5 minutes from arrest.
After sternal reopening → internal cardiac massage (two‑handed), internal defibrillation (20 J).

⚠️ 10‑day postoperative rule: resternotomy is recommended up to day 10. Later decisions by senior surgeon (adhesion risk).
*Based on STS (2017), ERC 2025, and CSU-ALS guidelines. Survival with CALS reaches 65–79% vs ~35% with standard ACLS.
⚙️ 6 CORE CALS ROLES + ACLS DIFFERENCES

✅ Role 1: External chest compressions (monitor pressure, depth) – until sternum open.
✅ Role 2: Airway / ventilation – FiO₂ 1.0, turn off PEEP, exclude pneumothorax.
✅ Role 3: Defibrillation / pacing – connect defibrillator, internal paddles, test epicardial wires.
✅ Role 4: Team leader (senior clinician) – runs protocol, decides resternotomy.
✅ Role 5: Medications – stop infusions, administer drugs (amiodarone, atropine, fluids).
✅ Role 6: ICU coordinator – mobilise surgeon, perfusionist, ECMO and resternotomy tray.

🚫 WHAT NOT TO DO (differences from standard ACLS):
• Do NOT give adrenaline routinely → may cause hypertension relapse, arrhythmias, increase myocardial demand.
• Do NOT start external compressions until rhythm/pacing assessed (first 60 sec).
• Do NOT use calcium or bicarbonate without specific indication (hyperK+, TCA overdose).
• No advantage of routine amiodarone over lidocaine.

🩸 Infusion support & notes

  • Crystalloids (warmed): 500–1000 mL bolus for hypovolaemia.
  • Phenylephrine / norepinephrine only after sternum open, for vasoplegia.
  • Epinephrine – NOT recommended in initial arrest (risk of afterload increase and bleeding).
  • If IABP in place → switch to trigger mode (pressure).
* Doses aligned with CALS 2025 and STS expert consensus.
🪡 EMERGENCY RESTERNOTOMY – STEP‑BY‑STEP
  • Call: Surgeon + perfusionist (prepare ECMO / MCS).
  • Kit: Sterile gloves, scalpel, scissors, retractor, clamps, internal paddles (5–20 J).
  • Step 1: Aseptic technique, rapid full‑length skin and subcutaneous incision.
  • Step 2: Cut or twist sternal wires (wire cutters) → open mediastinum.
  • Step 3: Evacuate clot, visualise heart, identify bleeding / tamponade.
  • Step 4: INTERNAL CARDIAC MASSAGE (two‑handed) – 100–120 /min, more effective than external.
  • Step 5: Internal defibrillation (20 J) → repeat if needed. Cross‑clamp or venous drainage for massive bleeding.
💡 Critical timeline: if after resternotomy and internal massage no ROSC, consider immediate VA‑ECMO (ECPR) – goal within 10–15 min from arrest.
🔄 MECHANICAL SUPPORT (MCS) & ECPR

For refractory arrest or no ROSC after resternotomy:
• Initiate VA‑ECMO (peripheral or central cannulation). Early start within 10–15 min improves survival.
• Consider Impella / IABP for left ventricular unloading in post‑cardiotomy cardiogenic shock.
• IABP can be a bridge if ECMO not available.
• In large centres, ECMO team is activated simultaneously with CALS call.

🧩 CALS + ECMO integration: Modern protocols include perfusionist and ECMO console mobilisation at time of “code C”. Designate ECMO coordinator as role #7.
📋 POST‑RESUSCITATION CARE AFTER OPEN CHEST

🫀 Immediate goals after ROSC:

  • Maintain MAP ≥65–70 mmHg (norepinephrine, vasopressin)
  • Control FiO₂: SpO₂ 92–96%, normocapnia (PaCO₂ 35–45 mmHg)
  • Monitor Hb, ABG, lactate, echocardiography (LV/RV function, tamponade)
  • Continue temporary pacing if needed; if persistent shock → IABP/ECMO

🌡️ Temperature control / neuroprognosis:

  • Active normothermia (36–37°C) for at least 24–48 hours in unresponsive patients.
  • Avoid hyperthermia.
  • Seizure surveillance (EEG). Neurofilament light chain (NfL) as biomarker.
  • Delayed sternal closure until haemodynamics and coagulopathy are stable.
*Sternal closure is often deferred – vacuum‑assisted temporary closure is common.
🔎 REVERSIBLE CAUSES (H’s & T’s) – ESPECIALLY RELEVANT AFTER STERNOTOMY
⚙️ Haemodynamic / Mechanical
  • Cardiac tamponade – #1 cause of failed external CPR
  • Hypovolaemia / massive bleeding
  • Tension pneumothorax (often iatrogenic from line placement)
  • Graft failure / coronary occlusion
  • Epicardial lead disconnection or poor capture
⚡ Metabolic / Arrhythmogenic
  • Severe electrolyte disorders (K⁺, Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺)
  • Hypoxaemia (circuit disconnect, ETT displacement)
  • Beta‑blocker / antiarrhythmic overdose
  • Graft spasm / thrombosis
→ If cause not identified within 2 min, proceed to resternotomy.
🧰 EMERGENCY RESTERNOTOMY BEDSIDE KIT
  • Sterile gloves, gown, cap, face shield
  • Scalpel #20, heavy scissors, Finocchietto retractor
  • Sternal wire cutters
  • Haemostatic clamps, electrocautery
  • Internal defibrillation paddles (20 J) + gel/saline pads
  • Spare epicardial pacing wires and generator
  • 50 mL syringes, warm saline irrigation
✅ CALS team: Roles are pre‑assigned. Quarterly simulation training is standard.
