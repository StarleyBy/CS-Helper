🚨 ACTIVE BLEEDING / LIFE‑THREATENING REVERSAL
⚠️ ANY ACTIVE BLEEDING (mild/moderate): Hold warfarin, IV vitamin K 5–10 mg, consider PCC 25–50 units/kg or FFP 15 mL/kg if PCC unavailable.
🆘 LIFE‑THREATENING BLEEDING / INTRACRANIAL HEMORRHAGE:
• IMMEDIATELY hold warfarin
• IV vitamin K 10 mg (slow over 30 min)
• 4‑factor PCC (Kcentra) 25–50 units/kg → target INR <1.5
• FFP 15–30 mL/kg if PCC not available (slower, volume overload risk)
• Repeat INR q15–30 min until <1.5.

🎯 TARGET INR & CLINICAL SCORES

Indication – target INR

  • Atrial fibrillation (non‑valvular): INR 2.0–3.0
  • Mechanical mitral/aortic valve (bileaflet): INR 2.5–3.5
  • Mechanical valve + additional risk (AF, LV thrombus): INR 3.0–4.0
  • DVT / PE / LV thrombus: INR 2.0–3.0
  • Bioprosthetic valve + AF: INR 2.0–3.0
🔹 CHA₂DS₂‑VASc (stroke risk, non‑rheumatic AF)
CHF (1), Hypertension (1), Age≥75 (2), DM (1), Stroke/TIA (2), Vascular disease (1), Age 65–74 (1), Female (1).
Anticoagulate if male ≥1, female ≥2.
🔹 HAS‑BLED (bleeding risk, score ≥3 high risk)
Hypertension (1), Abnormal renal/hepatic (1 or 2), Stroke (1), Bleeding history (1), Labile INR (1), Elderly >65 (1), Drugs/alcohol (1).
⚡ DRUG, FOOD & HERBAL INTERACTIONS (Appendix 11)
🔺 Increase INR (potentiation)
  • Amiodarone, Metronidazole
  • Fluconazole, Trimethoprim‑SMX
  • Simvastatin, Omeprazole
  • Diltiazem, Aspirin, Tramadol
  • Fish oils, Grapefruit, Mango
🔻 Decrease INR (inhibition)
  • Rifampin, Carbamazepine
  • Barbiturates, Cholestyramine
  • Nafcillin, Sucralfate
  • Avocado, Ginseng
  • Green leafy vegetables (high vitamin K)
  • Soy milk, Multivitamins with K
📌 Key advice: consistent vitamin K intake, avoid abrupt dietary changes. Inform all providers about warfarin.
📋 BRIDGING & PERIOPERATIVE MANAGEMENT

🔄 Interruption for surgery (mechanical valve / high risk)

  • Stop warfarin 5 days pre‑op
  • Start therapeutic LMWH (enoxaparin 1 mg/kg BID) when INR <2.0
  • Last LMWH 24h before procedure (12h for prophylactic dose)
  • Resume warfarin post‑op when hemostasis secure
  • Restart LMWH when INR <2.0 until INR therapeutic

🧠 Special considerations

  • Extreme INR >10 with no bleeding → oral vitamin K 2.5–5 mg, recheck in 24h.
  • Reversal with PCC and FFP: contraindicated in HIT history? use alternative.
  • If life‑threatening bleed and PCC not available → rFVIIa (off‑label, thrombotic risk).
  • After reversal: restart warfarin only when clinical stability regained and INR <1.5 (start with low dose).
⚙️ QUICK STEP‑BY‑STEP WARFarin INITIATION (wards/ICU)
✅ Step 1 – Assess

Baseline INR, CBC, LFTs, renal function. Identify sensitivity risks (age >75, low weight, amiodarone, Cr >1.5).

✅ Step 2 – Choose protocol

No risk factors → Standard (Day1: 5 mg). Sensitivity → Low‑dose (Day1: 2.5 mg).

✅ Step 3 – Daily INR & dosing

Follow day 1–6 tables above. Hold if INR >3.0, restart when <3.0 with reduced dose.

✅ Step 4 – Therapeutic range & maintenance

Once INR 2.0–3.0 (or target) for 2 days → adjust weekly doses (typical maintenance 2.5–10 mg/day).

⏱️ First 5 seconds summary: INITIATE with 5 mg or 2.5 mg (sensitive) → INR daily → REVERSAL: oral vitamin K if INR >5 without bleed; IV vitamin K + PCC for major/life‑threatening bleed.
🧾 PATIENT EDUCATION & DISCHARGE CHECKLIST
  • Take warfarin at same time (evening preferred, 5–6 PM).
  • Do not double dose; if missed, contact provider.
  • Avoid abrupt changes in green vegetables (keep vitamin K intake stable).
  • Report bleeding: hematuria, black stools, bruising, epistaxis, gingival bleeding.
  • Use soft toothbrush, electric razor, avoid NSAIDs unless approved.
  • Carry warfarin alert card and list of current medications.
  • Limit alcohol to ≤1 drink/day.
📞 Emergency contact: immediately seek care for severe headache, neurologic change, heavy bleeding, or chest trauma.
