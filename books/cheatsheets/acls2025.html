🫀 ACLS 2025 Quick Reference AHA Guidelines Update

Evidence-based algorithms for cardiac arrest, bradycardia, tachycardia & post-resuscitation care — compliant with 2025 AHA CPR/ECC science.
🆘 ADULT CARDIAC ARREST ALGORITHM (2025)
🔴 START CPR • 30:2 (or 100–120/min continuous)
  • High-quality chest compressions: depth 5–6 cm, full recoil, minimal interruptions
  • Apply defibrillator / monitor
⚡ RHYTHM CHECK
SHOCKABLE VF / pVT
→ Defibrillate (biphasic: 120-200 J, escalate as per device)
→ Resume CPR immediately (2 min)
→ IV/IO access (IV preferred, IO backup)
→ Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO q3-5min
→ After 2nd shock: Amiodarone 300 mg IV/IO (or Lidocaine 1-1.5 mg/kg)
→ Consider advanced airway if appropriate, minimize CPR breaks.
NON-SHOCKABLE PEA / Asystole
→ Resume CPR immediately
→ Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO q3-5min
→ Identify & treat reversible causes (H’s & T’s)
→ Consider point-of-care ultrasound if expertise available, without delaying compressions.
📌 Key 2025 updates: IV-first vascular access preferred (IO rescue). Antiarrhythmics after 2nd shock. Routine sodium bicarb, calcium, or magnesium NOT indicated unless specific condition (hyperK, TCA overdose). Mechanical CPR devices NOT routine.
*One‑shock strategy, immediate CPR post‑shock without pulse check. Rhythm check every 2 min. Ref: AHA ACLS 2025 Adult Cardiac Arrest
🐢 BRADYCARDIA with Pulse (2025)
1️⃣ Assess stability

HR typically <50/min + symptoms: hypotension, AMS, chest pain, acute HF, shock.

2️⃣ If UNSTABLE →
  • Atropine 1 mg IV bolus (repeat q3-5 min, max 3 mg)
  • If atropine ineffective → Transcutaneous pacing OR Dopamine infusion (5-20 mcg/kg/min) / Epinephrine infusion (2-10 mcg/min)
  • Prepare transvenous pacing
3️⃣ If STABLE → observe, treat underlying cause

Check electrolytes, thyroid, drugs; 12‑lead ECG.

⚕️ 2025 clarification: Atropine dose confirmed as 1 mg (not 0.5 mg). Dopamine/epinephrine infusions and transcutaneous pacing are rapid escalation options.
⚡ TACHYCARDIA with Pulse (2025)
🧠 Assess stability & narrow/wide QRS

Unstable: altered mental status, hypotension, chest pain, shock → IMMEDIATE SYNCHRONIZED CARDIOVERSION (start 50-100 J for narrow, 100-120 J for wide/monomorphic).

📊 Stable narrow QRS (≤120 ms)
  • Regular: Vagal maneuvers → Adenosine 6 mg rapid IV push; if no effect, 12 mg second dose. Consider beta-blocker/CCB.
  • Irregular (AF/AFL): Rate control (beta‑blockers, diltiazem, digoxin) unless pre‑excited AF → avoid AV nodal blockers.
🔲 Stable wide QRS (≥120 ms)
  • Assume VT until proven otherwise. Procainamide (20-50 mg/min) or amiodarone 150 mg IV over 10 min. If uncertain or fragile → cardioversion.
🚨 Pre‑excited AF (WPW): Avoid adenosine, verapamil, digoxin. Use procainamide or electrical cardioversion.
🩺 POST‑CARDIAC ARREST CARE • 2025 Updates (AHA 2025)

📋 Immediate Goals after ROSC

  • Maintain MAP ≥65 mm Hg (avoid hypotension)
  • Target SpO₂ 92-98% (avoid hyperoxia)
  • ETCO₂ normocapnia (35-45 mm Hg)
  • 12‑lead ECG & point-of-care echo ± CT for etiology
  • Coronary angiography recommended prior to discharge if suspected cardiac etiology, especially shockable rhythm or LV dysfunction.

🌡️ Temperature Control & Neuroprognostication

  • Active temperature control for at least 36 hours for unresponsive patients (TTM 32-36°C) → 2025 reaffirms 36h duration.
  • New biomarkers: Neurofilament light chain (NfL) and NSE for prognostic unfavorable outcome at 72h.
  • Treat seizures if EEG correlate — avoid suppressing myoclonus without EEG confirmation.
🧩 Survivorship: Structured assessment of emotional distress for patients and caregivers before discharge. Routine MCS (Impella, ECMO) not recommended except refractory cardiogenic shock selected cases.

🔄 REVERSIBLE CAUSES (H’s & T’s)
⚙️ H’s
  • Hypovolemia
  • Hypoxia
  • Hydrogen ion (acidosis)
  • Hypo-/hyperkalemia
  • Hypothermia
  • Hypoglycemia
⚡ T’s
  • Tension pneumothorax
  • Tamponade (cardiac)
  • Toxins / poisoning
  • Thrombosis (pulmonary, coronary)
  • Trauma
🔍 Use POCUS to rapidly identify tamponade, pneumothorax, or RV strain when feasible without interrupting CPR.
🤝 HIGH‑PERFORMANCE TEAM DYNAMICS
  • Clear leadership: closed‑loop communication.
  • Assign roles: compressor, airway, defib/drugs, timer/recorder.
  • CPR metrics: minimize peri‑shock pauses (<5 sec).
  • Brief rhythm checks every 2 minutes; don’t pause for pulse check after shock.
  • Post‑event debriefing (2025 emphasis to improve performance).
💡 2025 emphasis: “IV first, IO when needed”. Epinephrine given as soon as IV access achieved in non‑shockable rhythms; for shockable rhythms, give after second shock.
🚨 SPECIAL RESUSCITATION SITUATIONS

🤰 Cardiac Arrest in Pregnancy

  • Perimortem cesarean delivery (PMCD) considered by 4 minutes if no ROSC.
  • Left uterine displacement, high‑quality CPR, defibrillation safe.
  • Remove fetal monitors during defibrillation.

🧪 Opioid‑associated emergencies

  • Naloxone 0.4-2 mg IV/IM/IN, but CPR remains primary for cardiac arrest.
  • 2025 BLS/ACLS incorporates naloxone in respiratory/cardiac arrest if opioid overdose suspected.

⚠️ Electrolyte / toxicologic pearls

  • Hyperkalemia: IV calcium, insulin+glucose, albuterol.
  • TCA overdose: wide QRS → IV sodium bicarbonate (bolus 1-2 mEq/kg).
  • Magnesium sulfate: 2 g IV push for Torsade de Pointes (long QT).
*Refer to toxicology consultation for specific overdoses.
📌 2025 ACLS “What’s New” — Quick Summary
  • IV before IO when feasible; IO backup only if IV delayed.
  • Atropine for bradycardia: 1 mg (not 0.5 mg) .
  • Amiodarone or Lidocaine after 2nd shock, both acceptable.
  • Post‑arrest temperature control ≥ 36 hours .
  • Neurofilament light chain (NfL) added as prognostic biomarker.
  • Routine calcium / bicarb discouraged in undifferentiated arrest.

  • ⏱️ CPR cycles: 2 minutes between rhythm checks
  • 💉 Epinephrine: every 3–5 minutes
  • ⚡ Defibrillation: q2 min for refractory VF/pVT
  • 📉 Peri‑shock pause: aim <5 seconds
  • 💊 Amiodarone: after 2nd shock
