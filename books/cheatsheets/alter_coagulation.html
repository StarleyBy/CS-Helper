🩸 Direct Thrombin Inhibitors (IV) – Angiomax & Argatroban
💉 Bivalirudin (Angiomax®)

Indications: PCI, HIT/HITT (heparin‑induced thrombocytopenia), off‑label for ECMO/VAD anticoagulation.

SettingDosing
PCI (REPLACE‑2)0.75 mg/kg IV bolus → 1.75 mg/kg/h infusion for duration of procedure (up to 4h, then 0.2 mg/kg/h if needed)
HIT (non‑PCI, off‑label)0.15–0.2 mg/kg/h continuous infusion
Renal impairment (CrCl <30 mL/min)Reduce infusion by 20–25% (0.03–0.05 mg/kg/h)
ECMO / VAD (off‑label)Target aPTT 1.5–2.5× baseline. Start 0.05–0.1 mg/kg/h, titrate
  • Monitoring: aPTT (goal 1.5–2.5× baseline) or ACT (300–350 s for PCI). Draw 2–3h after start and after dose changes.
  • Half‑life: ~25 min; hepatic clearance preferred in renal failure.
  • Reversal: No specific antidote; short half‑life → discontinuation allows quick offset. In life‑threatening bleeding, consider recombinant factor VIIa or PCC (limited evidence).
💡 Key advantage: No risk of HIT, predictable dose‑response, and does not require antithrombin. Preferred over heparin in HIT patients and in centres with high ECMO volumes.
⚙️ Argatroban

Indications: HIT with or without thrombosis, PCI in HIT patients.

ParameterValue
Initial infusion (normal hepatic function)2 mcg/kg/min continuous IV
Max dose10 mcg/kg/min
Hepatic impairment (Child‑Pugh B/C)Start 0.5 mcg/kg/min
Critically ill or post‑cardiac surgery0.5–1.0 mcg/kg/min initial
PCI (bolus)350 mcg/kg over 3–5 min → infusion 25 mcg/kg/min (target ACT 300‑450 s)
  • Monitoring: aPTT (goal 1.5–3× baseline, max 100 s). Obtain baseline and 2h after start, then q4h.
  • Half‑life: 39–51 min, hepatically cleared.
  • Transition to warfarin: Overlap until INR is therapeutic on chromogenic factor Xa assay (argatroban falsely elevates INR).
⚠️ Warning: Argatroban prolongs INR; use chromogenic FXa assay when transitioning to VKA. No specific reversal agent; stop infusion (half‑life ~45 min).
💉 Fondaparinux (Arixtra®) – Parenteral Factor Xa Inhibitor

Mechanism: Synthetic pentasaccharide that selectively inhibits factor Xa via antithrombin. No routine monitoring required in most patients, but special populations may require anti‑Xa assay with fondaparinux calibrators.

  • Monitoring: Generally not required. In renal failure, obesity or pregnancy → measure anti‑FXa activity using a fondaparinux‑calibrated assay (target 0.5–1.5 mg/L). LMWH‑based anti‑Xa is not reliable (up to 40% difference).
  • Reversal: No specific antidote. For major bleeding, consider PCC (prothrombin complex concentrate) or FEIBA; rFVIIa provides partial correction. Andexanet alfa is not approved for fondaparinux. Investigational agent r‑antidote (PRT064445) may reverse it, but not clinically available.
  • Half‑life: ~17–21 h; renally excreted.
🧪 Important: Fondaparinux is not approved for use in HIT (off‑label with caution). It carries a small risk of HIT antibody formation.

🎯 Direct Thrombin Inhibitor (Oral)

Dabigatran (Pradaxa®) – oral DTI.

  • AF: 150 mg BID (≥80 yrs or at risk: 110 mg BID).
  • VTE treatment: 150 mg BID after 5–10 days parenteral.
  • Renal impairment: CrCl 30–50 mL/min: consider 110 mg BID; avoid if <30.

Monitoring: Routine coagulation tests not required; if needed, dilute thrombin time (dTT) or ecarin clotting time (ECT) provides accurate drug level assessment. Routine PT/aPTT is insensitive.

🌊 Iloprost (Aurlumyn® / Ventavis®) – Prostacyclin Analogue

Indications: Severe frostbite (IV), pulmonary arterial hypertension (inhaled), Raynaud’s phenomenon / critical limb ischaemia (off‑label). Iloprost exerts mild anticoagulant/antiplatelet effects via inhibition of platelet aggregation and vasodilation, used primarily as an adjunct in thrombotic microangiopathy and severe peripheral ischaemia.

Indication / RegimenDosing & titration
Severe frostbite (Aurlumyn®)Continuous IV infusion over 6 h daily for up to 8 days. Start 0.5 ng/kg/min, increase by 0.5 ng/kg/min q30 min according to tolerability up to max 2 ng/kg/min. Use actual body weight.
Pulmonary arterial hypertension (inhaled)2.5–5 mcg per inhalation, 6–9 times daily; not systemic anticoagulation
Hepatic impairment (Child‑Pugh B/C)Initiate at 0.25 ng/kg/min IV; same titration
  • Monitoring: Blood pressure, heart rate, signs of bleeding (headache, flushing, jaw pain, nausea are common dose‑limiting adverse effects). Discontinue infusion if dose‑limiting reactions persist; re‑start at previously tolerated rate.
  • Reversal: No specific antidote; short half‑life (~20–30 min). Management of bleeding is supportive.
  • Clinical pearl: Iloprost is not a conventional anticoagulant, but its anti‑aggregatory effect may increase bleeding risk when combined with other antithrombotics. Used in refractory thrombotic syndromes and as a vasodilator.
🧴 Preparation for IV use: Dilute 1 mL (100 mcg) in 100 mL 0.9% NaCl → final conc. 1 mcg/mL. Use 0.2 µm in‑line filter; stability 4h at room temperature.
🫀 Defibrotide (Defitelio®) – Hepatic VOD / SOS

Indication: Treatment of hepatic veno‑occlusive disease (sinusoidal obstruction syndrome, SOS) with renal or pulmonary dysfunction after hematopoietic stem‑cell transplantation (HSCT). Defibrotide has profibrinolytic, antithrombotic, and cytoprotective effects (not a classical anticoagulant).

ParameterDetails
Recommended dose6.25 mg/kg every 6 hours given as 2‑hour intravenous infusion. Based on baseline body weight prior to conditioning regimen.
DurationMinimum 21 days; continue until resolution of VOD/SOS, up to a maximum of 60 days.
Dose adjustments / toxicitiesWithhold for persistent severe bleeding; discontinue for anaphylaxis or recurrent major bleeding. No renal/hepatic dose adjustment known.
  • Monitoring: Daily bleeding assessment, complete blood count, coagulation parameters. Defibrotide does not require routine coagulation monitoring but may prolong aPTT slightly.
  • Reversal: No specific antidote. Half‑life ~24 min, supportive care for bleeding.
  • Peri‑procedure: Discontinue ≥2 h before invasive procedure; resume after bleeding risk resolves.
⚠️ Contraindications: Concurrent systemic anticoagulants (increases bleeding risk). Not for use in patients with known hypersensitivity or active major bleeding.

🧠 Clinical Algorithms & Special Situations
🩺 HIT management (non‑heparin anticoagulation)
  • Immediately stop all heparin (including flushes).
  • Start a direct thrombin inhibitor (bivalirudin or argatroban) or fondaparinux (off‑label). DOACs (apixaban, rivaroxaban) are now also used as simpler alternatives.
  • Do NOT use low‑molecular‑weight heparin or warfarin alone (warfarin can cause venous limb gangrene in acute HIT).
  • Transition to warfarin only after platelet recovery (>150,000/µL) and aPTT therapeutic on DTI. Overlap with DTI for ≥5 days, monitor chromogenic factor Xa for accurate INR.
💡 2025 update: New oral factor XI inhibitors (not yet widely available) may further reduce bleeding risk while preserving efficacy.
🫀 ECMO / VAD anticoagulation
  • Bivalirudin is increasingly used as first‑line agent for ECMO (predictable anticoagulation, no HIT, lower circuit thrombosis).
  • Target aPTT 1.5–2.5× baseline or ACT 180–220 s for VV ECMO, higher (200–250) for VA ECMO. Adjust based on bleeding/thrombosis.
  • Argatroban is alternative in hepatic impairment or where bivalirudin is contraindicated.
  • Frequent monitoring of aPTT (q4‑6h) and daily anti‑FXa levels not routinely required.
🧪 Renal & Hepatic dosing adjustments
  • Bivalirudin: Reduce dose by 20–25% in CrCl <30 mL/min; dose adjustment not needed for hepatic dysfunction.
  • Argatroban: Contraindicated in severe hepatic impairment (Child‑Pugh C); start 0.5 mcg/kg/min in moderate impairment.
  • Fondaparinux: Contraindicated if CrCl <30 mL/min. Use with caution 30–50. No hepatic adjustment.
  • DOACs: Apixaban and rivaroxaban have specific renal dose reductions; dabigatran avoid if CrCl <30 mL/min.
