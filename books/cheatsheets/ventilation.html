⚠️
All tidal volumes refer to Predicted Body Weight (PBW) — not actual body weight. Using actual weight in obese patients causes dangerous volutrauma.

🛡️
VILI has four mechanisms: volutrauma (volume), barotrauma (pressure), atelectrauma (repeated collapse/reopening), biotrauma (inflammatory cascade). Protective ventilation minimizes all four.

🚫 Avoid
  • Vt >8 mL/kg PBW in ARDS / at-risk lungs
  • Pplat >30 cmH₂O
  • Driving pressure >15 cmH₂O
  • FiO₂ >0.6 without escalating PEEP / recruitment
  • Derecruitment (unnecessary PEEP drops, disconnections)
  • Fluid overload in ARDS (conservative strategy)
  • Hyperoxia (PaO₂ >100 mmHg without indication)
🔢 Measuring ΔP
  1. Use VC-CMV or VSIMV (patient passive)
  2. Apply inspiratory hold 0.5–3 sec → read Pplat
  3. ΔP = Pplat − PEEP
  4. If ΔP >15 → ↓ Vt by 1 mL/kg steps or ↑ PEEP to ↑ compliance
  5. Reassess after each change
💡
ΔP is a proxy for lung strain. Low compliance → high ΔP at the same Vt. ΔP reveals true mechanical stress better than Vt alone. (Amato et al., NEJM 2015)

📌 ARDSnet low-Vt protocol
  • Vt = 6 mL/kg PBW (reduce to 4 if Pplat >30 or ΔP >15)
  • Pplat ≤30 cmH₂O
  • Driving pressure ≤15 cmH₂O
  • RR up to 35/min to maintain pH ≥7.20
  • PEEP/FiO₂ table (low- or high-PEEP strategy)
  • Target SpO₂ 92–96% or PaO₂ 55–80 mmHg
  • Conservative fluids post-resuscitation
  • Cisatracurium (NMB) 48 h if P/F <150 + significant asynchrony
  • Prone ≥16 h/day if P/F <150 after 12–24 h optimal settings

⚠️ Pathophysiology & recognition

Auto-PEEP (iPEEP) occurs when expiratory flow does not reach zero before the next breath — air is trapped, raising end-expiratory alveolar pressure above set PEEP.

Causes

  • COPD, asthma
  • High RR or short expiratory time
  • High Vt; small ETT; secretions
  • Dynamic airway collapse

Consequences

  • ↑ Intrathoracic pressure → ↓ venous return → hypotension / obstructive shock
  • Ineffective triggering (patient must overcome iPEEP first)
  • Dynamic hyperinflation → pneumothorax
  • ↑ Work of breathing
💡
Unexplained hypotension on ventilator in obstructive disease: suspect auto-PEEP first. Briefly disconnect → observe haemodynamic improvement and audible air release.
📏 Measurement & management

Measurement

Expiratory hold (0.5–2 sec) → read total PEEP.
Auto-PEEP = total PEEP − set PEEP

Management ladder

  • ↓ RR → ↑ expiratory time
  • ↓ Vt (less volume per breath to exhale)
  • ↑ I:E ratio (1:3–1:4)
  • ↑ Peak inspiratory flow (shortens Ti)
  • Bronchodilators + secretion clearance
  • Apply external PEEP ≈ 70–85% of measured iPEEP
🚨
In cardiac surgery patients: auto-PEEP severely impairs RV filling → haemodynamic collapse. Monitor actively in COPD or post-bronchospasm patients.
♾️
📖 Concept

Permissive hypercapnia = intentional acceptance of elevated PaCO₂ to achieve lower Vt and pressures — prioritizing lung protection over normocapnia.

  • Allow PaCO₂ up to 60–80 mmHg if haemodynamically tolerated
  • Acceptable pH: ≥7.20–7.25
  • Avoid rapid CO₂ fluctuations (arrhythmia risk)
  • NaHCO₃ may correct pH if <7.20 (controversial, tidal correction only)
💡
Modest hypercapnic acidosis may have direct lung-protective effects (anti-inflammatory). Generally well tolerated in ARDS with adequate haemodynamics.
🚫 Contraindications & cautions
  • Intracranial hypertension (TBI, post-neurosurgery) — CO₂ is a potent cerebral vasodilator; hypercapnia worsens ICP. Avoid.
  • Pulmonary hypertension — hypercapnia and acidosis ↑ PVR; worsens RV failure.
  • Severe metabolic acidosis (pH <7.20) — additional respiratory acidosis is dangerous.
  • RV failure post-cardiac surgery — maintain normocapnia; hypercapnia ↑ RV afterload.
  • Cardiac arrhythmias attributable to acidosis.
⚙️

Mechanical Power (MP) is the total energy delivered to the respiratory system per minute. It unifies volume, pressure, flow, and rate — capturing cumulative injurious potential better than any single parameter.

Simplified formula (VC-CMV)

MP (J/min) = 0.098 × RR × Vt(L) × (Ppeak − ΔP/2)

Pressures in cmH₂O · Vt in litres

Clinical thresholds (observational)

MP (J/min)Clinical implication
<12Generally safe zone
12–17Intermediate — minimize other risk factors
>17–20Associated with VILI and higher mortality in ARDS

Main contributors

  • High Vt (dominant driver)
  • High respiratory rate
  • High driving pressure
  • High PEEP (static component)
  • High airway resistance (↑ Ppeak)
💡
Hamilton C1 with INTELLiVENT-ASV can display estimated MP. Reducing RR and Vt synergistically lowers MP even when individual values appear acceptable.
🔬
MP is emerging and not yet in formal guidelines (2025), but provides a unified lens — especially when standard parameters conflict (e.g. low Vt but high RR + high PEEP). (Gattinoni et al., Intensive Care Med 2016)
❤️
🏥 Post-operative goals & fast-track
  • Early extubation (fast-track): target 4–8 h post-CABG / valve repair in uncomplicated cases. Reduces VAP, ICU stay, cost.
  • Protective ventilation even for short post-op: Vt 6–8 mL/kg PBW
  • SpO₂ 94–98% (liberal target acceptable with adequate cardiac output)
  • EtCO₂ 35–42 mmHg; avoid hypercapnia if elevated PVR suspected
  • PEEP 5–8 cmH₂O to prevent atelectasis (sternotomy + CPB effects)
  • Propofol or dexmedetomidine preferred over benzodiazepines
✅
Phrenic nerve palsy (post-IMA harvest, hypothermia): assess by ultrasound. Unilateral palsy may still permit extubation. Bilateral palsy requires continued support.
⚠️ Haemodynamic interactions with PPV
  • High PEEP → ↓ RV preload: in RV failure (post-tricuspid, PH, RV MI) — use lowest effective PEEP.
  • Hypercapnia and hypoxia → ↑ PVR → ↑ RV afterload: maintain normocapnia in RV failure.
  • Pulse pressure variation (PPV): valid fluid responsiveness marker only in fully controlled, arrhythmia-free ventilation with Vt ≥8 mL/kg — conflicts with lung protection in ARDS.
  • Post-pneumonectomy: PEEP 3–5 cmH₂O on remaining lung; minimize FiO₂; avoid high mean airway pressures.
📋 Extubation readiness criteria
  • Awake, follows commands
  • Spontaneous Vt >5 mL/kg PBW, RR <30/min
  • SpO₂ >92% on PS ≤8 cmH₂O + PEEP 5
  • FiO₂ ≤0.40, PEEP ≤8 cmH₂O
  • No vasopressor escalation
  • Adequate cough / secretion clearance
  • P0.1 <4–6 cmH₂O
💡
Post-extubation: consider HFNO prophylactically in impaired cardiac function to reduce re-intubation risk.
🚨 Delay extubation if:
  • Haemodynamic instability / vasopressor escalation
  • Significant bleeding requiring re-exploration
  • Uncontrolled arrhythmia (rapid AF)
  • Persistent severe hypoxemia (FiO₂ >0.5, PEEP >8)
  • Neurological deficit, prolonged obtundation
  • Severe metabolic acidosis / hypothermia
  • Bilateral phrenic palsy or diaphragm injury
✅
🟢 Indications for mechanical ventilation
  • Acute respiratory failure (PaO₂ <60 mmHg, PaCO₂ >50 with pH <7.25)
  • Apnoea / impending respiratory arrest
  • Severe hypoxemia (P/F <200)
  • ↑ Work of breathing (accessory muscles, paradoxical breathing, tachypnea)
  • Post-cardiac arrest / post-operative support
  • Neuromuscular disease (GBS, myasthenia gravis)
  • Airway protection failure
  • Transport — C1 is turbine-driven, no external gas source needed
🔴 Contraindications (relative)
  • Untreated pneumothorax (chest drain first)
  • Massive haemoptysis / severe distal airway obstruction
  • Advanced directive limiting life support
  • APRV contraindicated in severe obstructive disease (high auto-PEEP risk)
⚠️
C1 NIV mode contraindicated in: facial trauma, upper airway obstruction, inability to protect airway. Turbine compensates leaks up to 40 L/min.

⚠️
🚫 Ventilator errors
  • "High PEEP leak" — circuit leak or exhalation valve malfunction → check expiratory filter, reconnect, run SST
  • "Check patient circuit" — disconnection or occlusion → inspect circuit, replace wet filter
  • "Occlusion / high pressure" — secretions, ETT kink, biting → suction, check ETT position, sedation
  • "Power failure" — battery depleted → connect external power; C1 has 4-h hot-swappable battery
  • "Sensor error" — contaminated flow/pressure sensor → run leak test, replace sensor
⚠️ Clinical warnings
  • "Low compliance" — atelectasis, ARDS, pneumothorax → recruit, CXR
  • "Auto-PEEP high" — COPD/asthma → ↓ RR, ↑ Te, bronchodilators, apply external PEEP ≈70–85% iPEEP
  • "Asynchrony detected" — double-triggering or ineffective efforts → adjust trigger, consider mode change
  • "High FiO₂ demand" — FiO₂ >0.6 prolonged → optimize PEEP, recruitment, prone, treat cause
🧰
"Ventilator inoperative": Disconnect and manually bag immediately. Always have backup device at bedside.

Mechanism: Integrates IBW, minute ventilation target, and SpO₂/EtCO₂ feedback to auto-adjust RR, PS, PEEP, and FiO₂ breath-to-breath.

✅ Evidence-based weaning approach

Daily Spontaneous Awakening Trials (SAT) + Spontaneous Breathing Trials (SBT) are the recommended strategy (Girard 2008; ACCCM/ERS 2017). Superior to gradual SIMV rate reduction.

  • SAT: interrupt sedation daily until awake/arousable
  • SBT: PSV 5–8 cmH₂O + PEEP 5, or CPAP/T-piece for 30–120 min
  • Pass criteria: RR <35, SpO₂ >92%, Vt >5 mL/kg PBW spontaneous, no distress, haemodynamically stable
  • Pass → extubate · Fail → full support, reassess daily
⚠️
Gradual SIMV rate reduction is not recommended as a primary weaning strategy — it is slower and associated with worse outcomes. Use SIMV for partial support in pre-SBT patients only.
🔁 Readiness checklist
  • Underlying cause of respiratory failure treated / improving
  • FiO₂ ≤0.40, PEEP ≤8 cmH₂O
  • Haemodynamically stable (no or minimal vasopressors)
  • Intact cough / secretion clearance
  • Awake, follows simple commands
  • P0.1 <4–6 cmH₂O (adequate drive, no excessive demand)
💡
Post-extubation in cardiac surgery or high-risk patients: consider HFNO prophylactically to reduce re-intubation risk.
