⚡ Special modes & features
  • PRVC (Pressure Regulated Volume Control) – pressure‑limited, volume‑targeted.
  • APRV – for refractory hypoxemia; set P_high, T_high, P_low, T_low.
  • CPAP / PSV – for weaning and spontaneous breathing trials.
  • VSIMV+PSIMV tip: Always set backup rate, appropriate PS for spontaneous breaths (5–15 cmH₂O). Adjust SIMV rate to offload respiratory muscles (start 6–12/min).
💡 Hamilton C1 unique: INTELLiVENT‑ASV uses minute ventilation, ideal body weight, and SpO₂/EtCO₂ targets to adjust RR, PEEP, and pressure support automatically.
📊 Ventilator parameters: definitions, effects, and clinical titration
🫀 Basic parameters (what they are & how to adjust)
ParameterDescriptionPhysiologic effectWhen to adjust (typical values)
Tidal Volume (Vt)Volume delivered per breath (mL or mL/kg PBW)Affects alveolar ventilation (PaCO₂) and lung stretch (volutrauma)6–8 mL/kg PBW in ARDS; 8–10 mL/kg in obstructive disease; monitor plateau pressure ≤30 cmH₂O
Respiratory Rate (RR)Breaths per minute (mandatory + spontaneous)Directly controls minute ventilation → PaCO₂Start 12–16, adjust to achieve normocapnia (PaCO₂ 35–45). In SIMV: set backup rate 4–8 below spontaneous rate.
PEEP (Positive End‑Expiratory Pressure)Pressure remaining in airways at end‑expirationRecruits alveoli, improves oxygenation, prevents atelectasisARDS: 5–15 cmH₂O (higher with FiO₂). Obstructive: ≤5 cmH₂O to avoid hyperinflation.
FiO₂Fraction of inspired oxygen (0.21–1.0)Increases PaO₂, but prolonged high FiO₂ causes absorption atelectasis / toxicityTitrate to SpO₂ 88–92% (ARDS) or 92–96% (others). Use lowest possible FiO₂ to avoid toxicity.
Inspiratory Pressure (ΔP / PS)Pressure above PEEP during inspirationDetermines delivered tidal volume in pressure‑controlled modes and PSVStart 10–15 cmH₂O above PEEP; adjust to achieve Vt 6–8 mL/kg. Keep plateau pressure <30.
I:E ratioInspiratory to expiratory time ratioAffects mean airway pressure, gas trapping, and ventilation distributionNormal 1:2; prolonged I:E (1:1 or inverse) for ARDS to improve oxygenation.
Flow (inspiratory)Peak inspiratory flow rate (L/min)Higher flow shortens inspiratory time, reduces patient effortTypically 40–60 L/min; obstructive disease: 60–80 L/min to allow prolonged expiration.
Trigger sensitivity (flow/pressure)Effort needed to initiate assisted breathToo sensitive → auto‑trigger; insensitive → increased work of breathingFlow trigger: 2–5 L/min; Pressure: –1 to –2 cmH₂O. Adjust to avoid asynchrony.
🧠 Clinical pearl for SIMV (VSIMV/PSIMV): Start with mandatory rate 10–12/min, PS 10–15 cmH₂O, Vt 6–8 mL/kg (for VSIMV). Wean by reducing mandatory rate by 2–4 breaths/min every 2–4 hours, then transition to CPAP/PSV when rate ≤4–6/min.

✅ Indications & ⚠️ Contraindications for mechanical ventilation (C1 specific)
🟢 Indications
  • Acute respiratory failure (PaO₂ <60 mmHg, PaCO₂ >50 mmHg with pH <7.25)
  • Apnoea or impending respiratory arrest
  • Severe hypoxemia (PaO₂/FiO₂ <200)
  • Increased work of breathing (accessory muscle use, tachypnea, paradoxical breathing)
  • Post‑cardiac arrest / post‑operative respiratory support
  • Neuromuscular disease (Guillain‑Barré, myasthenia gravis)
  • Transport ventilation (C1 lightweight, turbine‑driven, no external air source needed)
🔴 Contraindications (relative)
  • Untreated pneumothorax (needs chest tube prior to PPV)
  • Massive haemoptysis / severe airway obstruction distal to ETT
  • Advanced directive limiting life support (withdrawal of care)
  • Some modes (APRV) not suitable for severe obstructive lung disease due to intrinsic PEEP risk.
⚠️ C1 specific caution: In NIV mode, contraindicated in facial trauma, upper airway obstruction, or inability to protect airway. Turbine can compensate for leaks up to 40 L/min.

⚠️ Common errors, warnings & troubleshooting (C1)
🚫 Ventilator errors
  • “High PEEP leak” – circuit leak or exhalation valve malfunction → check expiratory filter, reconnect circuit, run SST (short self test).
  • “Check patient circuit” – disconnection or occlusion → inspect entire circuit, replace filter if wet.
  • “Occlusion / high pressure” – secretions, ETT kink, biting → suction, check ETT position, consider sedation.
  • “Power failure” – internal battery depleted → connect to external power; C1 has 4‑hour hot‑swappable battery.
  • “Sensor error” – flow or pressure sensor contaminated → run leak test, replace flow sensor if needed.
⚠️ Clinical warnings & decision support
  • “Low compliance” – drop in dynamic compliance (norm 50–100 mL/cmH₂O). Causes: atelectasis, ARDS, pneumothorax → recruit, chest X‑ray.
  • “Auto‑PEEP high” – incomplete expiration, especially in asthma/COPD. Reduce RR, increase expiratory time, give bronchodilators.
  • “Asynchrony detected” – double‑triggering or ineffective efforts → adjust flow trigger, change mode, consider pressure support increase.
  • “High FiO₂ demand” – FiO₂ >60% for extended period → evaluate PEEP recruitment, consider prone positioning, treat underlying pathology.
🧰 Immediate action: For any “Ventilator inoperative” error, disconnect patient and resuscitate with manual bag. Always have a backup ventilation device ready.
📝 Quick initial setup (adult, IBW 70 kg)

💨 VSIMV (Volume SIMV)

  • Mandatory Vt: 420–500 mL (6–8 mL/kg)
  • SIMV rate: 10–12/min (backup)
  • Pressure Support (PS): 10–15 cmH₂O for spontaneous breaths
  • PEEP: 5 cmH₂O
  • FiO₂: 1.0 → titrate to SpO₂ 92–96%
  • Flow: 50 L/min (square wave)
  • Trigger: flow 3 L/min
📌 Weaning: reduce SIMV rate gradually as spontaneous breathing improves.

🫁 PSIMV (Pressure SIMV)

  • Pressure control (ΔP): 12–15 cmH₂O above PEEP → target Vt 6–8 mL/kg
  • SIMV rate: 10–12/min
  • Pressure Support (PS): 10–15 cmH₂O for spontaneous breaths
  • PEEP: 5–8 cmH₂O
  • Inspiratory time: 1.0–1.2 sec
  • FiO₂: titrate
📌 Ideal for COPD: use lower SIMV rate (6–8/min) and higher inspiratory flow to allow prolonged expiration.
📌 ARDSnet protocol: low Vt (6 mL/kg PBW), limit plateau pressure ≤ 30 cmH₂O, PEEP/FiO₂ table. Use APRV or INTELLiVENT‑ASV for difficult oxygenation.
🧪 Advanced mode deep dive: INTELLiVENT‑ASV & APRV
🤖 INTELLiVENT‑ASV (closed‑loop)

How it works: Uses ideal body weight, minute ventilation target, and SpO₂/EtCO₂ to automatically adjust RR, PS, PEEP, and FiO₂.

  • Targets: EtCO₂ 35–45 mmHg, SpO₂ 88–96%
  • Safety limits: Pmax 40, PEEP max 20, RR max 40
  • When to use: Weaning, stable ARDS, post‑op, neuro patients. Avoid in severe metabolic acidosis.
💡 Monitor patient‑ventilator synchrony; if frequent apnoea or high RR, adjust %MinVol target upward.
⏱️ APRV (Airway Pressure Release Ventilation)

Key parameters:

  • P_high (20–30), T_high (4–6 sec)
  • P_low (0–5), T_low (0.2–0.8 sec)
  • Release breaths occur by dropping to P_low for short T_low.

Indication: Severe ARDS (PaO₂/FiO₂ < 150) unresponsive to conventional ventilation. Improves oxygenation via recruitment.

⚠️ Contraindicated in severe obstructive lung disease (air trapping), large air leak, elevated ICP.
🧠 Clinical notes on VSIMV/PSIMV: These SIMV‑based modes are essential for partial ventilatory support. Use PSIMV when you need to limit airway pressures (e.g., COPD, asthma, post‑pneumonectomy). Use VSIMV for patients requiring guaranteed minute ventilation (e.g., neurological weakness). Always set the backup rate below the patient's spontaneous rate to avoid stacking. Monitor for breath‑stacking, auto‑triggering, and ineffective efforts – adjust trigger and flow accordingly.
