# ECG Normal Values

## Intervals & Durations

| Parameter | Normal Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Heart rate | 60 – 100 bpm | Sinus rhythm |
| PR interval | 120 – 200 ms | AV conduction |
| QRS duration | < 120 ms | > 120 ms = bundle branch block |
| QTc (Bazett) | ♂ < 440 ms · ♀ < 460 ms | Corrected for HR |
| QTc prolonged | > 500 ms | High risk |
| P wave duration | < 120 ms | |
| P wave amplitude | < 2.5 mm | II, aVF |

## Axis

| Axis | Degrees | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| Normal | −30° to +90° | |
| Left axis deviation | < −30° | LAHB, inferior MI, LVH |
| Right axis deviation | > +90° | RVH, LPHB, lateral MI |
| Extreme RAD | > +180° | Serious pathology |

## P Wave

- Normal morphology: upright in I, II, aVF; biphasic V1
- **P mitrale**: notched P in I, II; width > 120 ms → LA enlargement
- **P pulmonale**: tall, peaked P in II; amplitude > 2.5 mm → RA enlargement

## QRS Morphology

### LVH criteria (Sokolow-Lyon)
- S in V1 + R in V5 or V6 > **35 mm**
- R in aVL > **11 mm**

### RVH criteria
- R > S in V1; R in V1 > 7 mm
- Right axis deviation

### LBBB
- QRS > 120 ms; broad notched R in I, V5, V6; rS or QS in V1

### RBBB
- QRS > 120 ms; RSR' (M pattern) in V1; wide S in I, V6

## ST Segment

| Finding | Criteria | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| ST elevation | > 1 mm limb leads; > 2 mm precordial | STEMI, pericarditis, Brugada |
| ST depression | ≥ 0.5 mm | Ischemia, NSTEMI, digoxin |
| J-point elevation | Common in young males | Usually benign |

## T Wave

- Normally upright in I, II, V3–V6
- Inverted T in III, aVR, V1 — normal variant
- **Wellens syndrome**: deep symmetric T inversion V2–V3 → LAD stenosis

## QTc Prolongation — Common Causes

- **Drugs**: amiodarone, sotalol, quinidine, haloperidol, azithromycin
- **Electrolytes**: hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia, hypocalcemia
- **Congenital**: LQTS
