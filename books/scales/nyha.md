# NYHA Functional Classification of Heart Failure

## Classes

| Class | Definition | Symptoms |
|:---:|---|---|
| **I** | No limitation of physical activity | Ordinary activity does NOT cause symptoms |
| **II** | Slight limitation | Comfortable at rest; ordinary activity → fatigue, palpitation, dyspnea |
| **III** | Marked limitation | Comfortable at rest; less than ordinary activity → symptoms |
| **IV** | Unable to carry on any activity without discomfort | Symptoms at rest; discomfort with any activity |

## NYHA vs ACC/AHA Stages

| ACC/AHA Stage | Description | NYHA Class |
|:---:|---|:---:|
| A | High risk; no structural disease, no symptoms | — |
| B | Structural disease; no symptoms | I |
| C | Structural disease with prior or current symptoms | I – IV |
| D | Refractory HF requiring special interventions | IV |

## Prognostic Correlates

| NYHA | 1-year mortality | 5-year mortality |
|:---:|---|---|
| I–II | ~5–10% | ~25–35% |
| III | ~15–20% | ~50% |
| IV | ~30–70% | ~80–90% |

## Treatment Targets by NYHA

- **Class I–II:** ACEI/ARB + beta-blocker + MRA if EF ≤ 35%; SGLT2i
- **Class III–IV:** Add diuretics; consider CRT if QRS ≥ 130 ms + LBBB; evaluate for ICD, LVAD, transplant
- **NYHA IV refractory:** Palliative care discussion; inotrope bridge; transplant/LVAD evaluation
