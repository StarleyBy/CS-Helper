# HAS-BLED Bleeding Risk Score

Estimates 1-year risk of major bleeding in patients on anticoagulation for AF.

## Scoring

| Criterion | Points |
|---|:---:|
| **H** — Hypertension (uncontrolled SBP > 160 mmHg) | 1 |
| **A** — Abnormal renal OR liver function (1 pt each) | 1–2 |
| **S** — Stroke history | 1 |
| **B** — Bleeding history or predisposition | 1 |
| **L** — Labile INR (TTR < 60%) | 1 |
| **E** — Elderly (age > 65) | 1 |
| **D** — Drugs (antiplatelet / NSAIDs) OR alcohol (1 pt each) | 1–2 |

**Maximum score: 9**

## Interpretation

| Score | Risk | Annual bleeding rate |
|:---:|---|---|
| 0–1 | Low | ~1% |
| 2 | Moderate | ~1.9% |
| ≥ 3 | High | ~3.7–8.7% |

> **Important:** High HAS-BLED score should prompt correction of modifiable bleeding risk factors (BP control, stopping NSAIDs, addressing alcohol), **not** automatic withholding of anticoagulation. The benefit of stroke prevention generally outweighs bleeding risk in most patients with CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥ 2.

## Modifiable Risk Factors to Address
- Uncontrolled hypertension → optimize BP
- Labile INR → switch to NOAC
- Antiplatelet / NSAID use → discontinue if possible
- Excessive alcohol → counseling
