# Insulin Basal-Bolus Protocol

---

## 1. Background & Indications

Hyperglycemia is present in up to 45% of inpatients and is associated with worse outcomes, including increased infections, length of stay, and mortality. The **basal‑bolus regimen** is the preferred standard of care for most non‑critically ill hospitalized patients with diabetes or persistent hyperglycemia (blood glucose ≥180 mg/dL).

**Indications in cardiac surgery & CICU:**

- Pre‑existing type 1 or type 2 diabetes
- New‑onset persistent hyperglycemia (>180 mg/dL) on two or more occasions
- Stress hyperglycemia during acute illness or after cardiac surgery
- Transition from intravenous insulin infusion to subcutaneous therapy

---

## 2. Glycemic Targets (mg/dL)

| Patient population | Glucose target (mg/dL) | Level of evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Most non‑critically ill inpatients | 110–180 | ADA 2026 (B) |
| Perioperative period (ADA 2026) | 100–180 | ADA 2026 |
| Early post‑cardiac surgery (first 24 h) | 140–180 | EACTS 2024 (I,B) |
| Cardiac ICU (stable, enteral/oral intake) | 110–180 | EACTS 2024 (I,B) |

> **Note:** Individualize targets in frail patients, severe CKD, or frequent hypoglycemia. Avoid strict control (<110 mg/dL) in critically ill patients.

---

## 3. Initial Dose Calculation (Weight‑Based)

Use the interactive **Insulin Basal-Bolus Calculator** (companion HTML tool).

### Step 1 — Estimate Total Daily Dose (TDD)

TDD = Weight (kg) × Factor (U/kg)

| Clinical context | Factor (U/kg) |
|---|---|
| Insulin‑naïve, HbA1c <7% | 0.3 |
| Moderate control (HbA1c 7–9%) | 0.4 |
| Poor control (HbA1c >9%) | 0.5 |

### Step 2 — Adjust for renal function

| eGFR (mL/min) | TDD adjustment |
|---|---|
| ≥60 (normal) | 100% |
| 30–59 (mild‑moderate CKD) | 80–85% |
| 15–29 (severe CKD) | 65–70% |
| <15 (dialysis / CRRT) | 40–50% |

### Step 3 — Split doses

- **Basal insulin (50% of TDD):** Glargine U‑100, Detemir, or Degludec once daily (same time each day)
- **Bolus insulin (50% of TDD):** Rapid‑acting analogue (Aspart, Lispro, Glulisine) divided equally before **breakfast, lunch, and dinner**

### Step 4 — Correction (supplemental) scale

Rapid‑acting analogue **added** to the scheduled bolus if pre‑meal glucose exceeds target.

| Pre‑meal glucose (mg/dL) | Additional units |
|---|---|
| 146–180 | +1 |
| 181–220 | +2 |
| 221–260 | +3 |
| 261–300 | +4 |
| >300 | +5 (notify MD) |

## 4. Special Situations — Cardiac Surgery

### 4.1 Preoperative (elective cardiac surgery)

- Perform HbA1c in all patients with diabetes **within 3 months prior to surgery**
- Target preoperative glucose **100–180 mg/dL** and HbA1c <8% (ADA 2026)
- Continue basal insulin on morning of surgery (reduce to 50–80% of usual dose)
- Hold oral hypoglycemics (metformin day before, SGLT2 inhibitors 72 h before)

### 4.2 Intraoperative

- **Target glucose:** <180 mg/dL (EACTS 2024, Class I, B)
- Use **intravenous insulin infusion** for all diabetic patients undergoing cardiopulmonary bypass (CPB)
- Start IV insulin when glucose >180 mg/dL

### 4.3 Postoperative — Transition from IV to Subcutaneous Insulin

| Timepoint | Action |
|---|---|
| **First 24 h post‑op (ICU)** | Continue IV insulin targeting **140–180 mg/dL** |
| **When oral intake resumes** | Calculate **24‑hour IV insulin requirement** from stable period |
| **Transition dose** | Give **50% of the 24‑hour IV requirement** as subcutaneous basal insulin. Hold IV insulin 2–4 h after first subcutaneous dose |
| **Resume bolus insulin** | Start with meals at **50% of calculated bolus** on day one |

> **EACTS 2024 recommendation:** Start with **50% of the preoperative TDD** or the calculated dose, then titrate.

### 4.4 Cardiac Surgery — NPO / delayed oral intake

- Continue **50–75% of basal insulin** daily
- Hold all bolus insulin
- Monitor glucose **every 4 hours**
- Add correction doses of rapid‑acting insulin if glucose >180 mg/dL per correction scale

---

## 5. Special Situations — Cardiac ICU & General Wards

### 5.1 Enteral or parenteral nutrition

- **Continuous enteral feeding:** Give **50% of TDD as basal insulin**; the remainder as **regular insulin 6‑hourly** or rapid‑acting analogue 4‑hourly
- **Parenteral nutrition (PN):** Add **regular insulin directly to PN bag** (start with 1 U per 10 g carbohydrate) plus **basal insulin** 50% of TDD

### 5.2 Corticosteroid‑induced hyperglycemia

- Patients on high‑dose steroids often require **30–50% higher TDD**
- Consider **twice‑daily basal insulin** (e.g., NPH or Glargine BID) to cover afternoon/evening steroid effect

### 5.3 Renal replacement therapy (CRRT / HD)

- **CRRT (citrate anticoagulation):** Insulin requirements may decrease significantly; start with **lowest factor (0.3 U/kg)** and monitor glucose every **2 hours**
- **Hemodialysis:** Consider holding morning bolus insulin if pre‑dialysis glucose is <200 mg/dL; give **50% of basal** on dialysis days

### 5.4 Type 1 diabetes (T1DM)

- Patients with T1DM **must never** have insulin withdrawn completely (risk of DKA)
- Continue **basal insulin** even if NPO
- Use **only rapid‑acting analogues** for prandial/correction doses

### 5.5 Hypoglycemia management (mg/dL)

| Glucose (mg/dL) | Action |
|---|---|
| **55–69** | 15 g oral glucose; recheck in 15 min |
| **50–54** | 20 g oral glucose or 50 mL D50W IV; recheck in 15 min |
| **<50** | Treat as severe: 25 g IV D50W (or 1 mg glucagon). Notify MD immediately. |

---

## 6. Monitoring Frequency

| Phase | Glucose monitoring |
|---|---|
| **Initial dose start / significant dose change** | Before each meal and at bedtime (4× daily) |
| **Stable (2 consecutive days in target)** | Before breakfast and before dinner (2× daily) |
| **NPO / enteral feeding** | Every 4 h |
| **CICU (IV insulin infusion)** | Hourly per institutional protocol |
| **CRRT** | Every 1–2 h |

**Additional laboratory monitoring:**
- **HbA1c** on admission if not measured in preceding 3 months
- **Basic metabolic panel** daily during active insulin titration (risk of hypokalemia)
- **Point‑of‑care glucose** for all corrections

---

## 7. Dose Titration Algorithm

Adjust TDD every **1–2 days** based on glycemic trends.

| Glucose pattern over 24 h | Action |
|---|---|
| **Fasting consistently >130 mg/dL** | Increase basal insulin by **10–20%** |
| **Pre‑lunch or pre‑dinner consistently >160 mg/dL** | Increase pre‑meal bolus by **10–20%** |
| **Post‑meal (2 h) consistently >200 mg/dL** | Increase pre‑meal bolus by **10–20%** |
| **Any glucose <70 mg/dL** | Decrease the insulin dose given within 4 h of the event by **20%** |
| **Two or more hypoglycemic events in 24 h** | Reduce TDD by **20%** and re‑evaluate |

---

## 8. Transition to Outpatient Care (Israel‑specific)

- Provide a **discharge insulin regimen** at least **24 h before discharge**
- For patients with newly initiated insulin, prescribe **basal + bolus** insulin at the **last stable inpatient doses**
- Ensure a **follow‑up appointment** with primary care or endocrinology within **1–2 weeks** (via Maccabi, Clalit, Leumit, or Meuhedet)
- Provide **diabetes education** (insulin injection technique, hypoglycemia recognition, glucose monitoring)
- Prescribe glucagon kit for patients with T1DM or high‑dose insulin (>50 U/day)

**Insulin Basal-Bolus – 10 Key Points**

6. **Correction scale (add to meal bolus):**  
   146–180 → +1 U 181–220 → +2 U 221–260 → +3 U 261–300 → +4 U >300 → +5 U
