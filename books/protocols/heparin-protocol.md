# Heparin Anticoagulation Protocol for Cardiac Surgery

> For interactive dose calculator see **Calculators → Heparin Infusion (Cardiac Protocol)**

## Standard UFH Preparation

| Concentration | Preparation | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| 50 U/mL | 25,000 U in 500 mL NS | Standard infusion (most common) |
| 100 U/mL | 25,000 U in 250 mL NS | Fluid‑restricted patients |
| 1 U/mL | 500 U in 500 mL NS | Arterial line flush |

## Indications (post‑cardiac surgery)

- After CABG, valve surgery, or combined procedures
- Bridge anticoagulation for mechanical valves / LVAD
- Post‑op VTE prevention / treatment (DVT, PE)
- Renal failure (CKD stage ≥4) – preferred over LMWH

## Initial Dosing – Weight‑Based Table (no weight‑based formula)

Start infusion according to patient’s actual body weight **without** loading bolus (unless specified).  
If a bolus is ordered: **50–75 U/kg IV** (round to nearest 100 U).

| Body weight | Initial infusion rate |
|---|---|
| 40–60 kg | 600 U/h |
| 61–70 kg | 800 U/h |
| 71–80 kg | 1000 U/h |
| 81–90 kg | 1100 U/h |
| 91–100 kg | 1200 U/h |
| > 100 kg | 1500 U/h |

> *First aPTT check:* **6 hours** after start (or **4 hours** if a bolus was given).

## Monitoring Schedule

- **aPTT:**  
  - 6h after initiation  
  - 6h after each rate change  
  - When two consecutive values are therapeutic → check **daily (AM)**
- **Platelet count:**  
  - Baseline before heparin  
  - **Daily** if <100,000/µL  
  - **Every other day** if >100,000/µL while on heparin  
- **Guaiac all stools** – notify house officer if positive  
- Notify MD for any bleeding, aPTT <35 or >100 seconds

## aPTT‑Based Dose Adjustment (Cardiac Surgery Nomogram)

**Target aPTT: 56–65 seconds** (laboratory‑specific; adjust if local normal range differs)

| aPTT (sec) | Action | Rate change (U/kg/h) | Recheck aPTT |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 46 | Increase rate | +4 U/kg/h | 4h |
| 46–55 | Increase rate | +2 U/kg/h | 4h |
| **56–65** | **No change – therapeutic** | — | 8h |
| 66–75 | Decrease rate | –1 U/kg/h | 6h |
| 76–90 | Decrease rate | –2 U/kg/h | 4h |
| 91–100 | Stop 1 hour, then decrease | –3 U/kg/h | 4h |
| > 100 | Stop 2 hours, then decrease | –4 U/kg/h | 4h |

> **Important:** After stopping infusion for high aPTT, resume at the new calculated rate (reduced by the indicated U/kg/h).

## Special Situations

### Pre‑Procedure (non‑emergent)
- Stop infusion **4–6 hours** before procedure  
- Check aPTT before restarting  
- Resume at previous maintenance rate (no bolus) unless INR <2

### Early Post‑operative (within 4–6 hours)
- Do **not** restart UFH until surgical hemostasis confirmed  
- If needed, start without bolus at **10–15 U/kg/h** (lower target aPTT 50–65 sec)

### HIT (Heparin‑Induced Thrombocytopenia)
- **Suspect:** Platelet drop >50% from baseline, thrombosis, timing day 5–14  
- Calculate **4Ts score**  
- **Stop all heparin immediately** (including flushes)  
- Switch to **Argatroban** (hepatic metabolism, preferred in renal failure) or **Fondaparinux**  
- Do **not** start warfarin until platelets >150 × 10⁹/L

## Reversal (Protamine Sulfate)

- **1 mg protamine per 100 U heparin** received in the **last 2–3 hours**  
- Maximum single dose: **50 mg**  
- Administer **slow IV over 10 minutes** (risk of hypotension, bradycardia, anaphylaxis)  
- If last heparin dose >3 hours ago: reversal is rarely needed (t½ 60–90 min)

## Transition to Warfarin (if indicated)

- Start warfarin when aPTT is therapeutic (56–65 sec)  
- Overlap heparin + warfarin for **minimum 5 days** AND until INR ≥2.0 for two consecutive days  
- Then discontinue heparin

---

## Important Reminders

- **Discontinue all previous heparin orders** before starting this protocol.  
- Do **not** administer heparin within **12 hours** after the last dose of low‑molecular‑weight heparin (LMWH).  
- Baseline labs before start: **PT, aPTT, CBC, platelet count**.  
- Daily aPTT in AM once stable.  
- Notify house officer for **any bleeding**, **aPTT <35 sec**, or **>100 sec**.
